**Subject:** Skincare brand — how I'd think about it

---

Hey Saad,

Been thinking about the skincare project since we talked and wanted to share how I'd approach it before quoting anything. Easier to align on deliverables first.

What I heard: new DTC skincare brand, products mostly done. You want it built to convert from day one and AI baked into how landing pages get generated and tested. Short-term we get something live and selling; long-term we have a growth system that compounds.

I'd think about it in two phases. First a launch sprint — finish as much of the brand and design as possible, ship the store, set up analytics so we're not flying blind on day one. Then an ongoing growth engagement where the AI piece lives.

For the ongoing piece, we'd ship two conversion-tested landing pages per week, each built around a new angle (ingredient, concern, demographic, testimonial). Your paid team gets fresh LPs every week without designing from scratch. We can flex the cadence based on your paid budget.

Plus we can figure some weekly or bi-weekly meetings to speak about analytics, offer testing, ideas to try, etc.

To do this right I'd need a team. Martin (my partner whom I work with on these type of jobs, runs delivery), plus a senior brand designer and a developer. I can look for them or you can, happy either way. Either way I'd want them on our side of the work so accountability stays clean.

Before I can come back with real numbers, a few things I'd want to know:

— Launch date you're aiming for, and what's driving it (paid budget, PR, internal milestone) or is it flexible
— Where's the brand at: name, logo, packaging, voice. What's locked, what's open
— SKUs at launch, roughly what price range
— The AI landing-page side you mentioned: what would you want out of it?
— After launch: retainer with us for CRO + website strategy + LP creation, or building that in-house?
— Budget shape: easier to greenlight a fixed project fee, a monthly retainer, or a mix
— Who's running paid acquisition: existing team, agency, or hiring

Not sure if I mentioned it on our call, but on the side Martin and I have been building an AI app that generates hundreds of ad creatives, almost for free.

Apart from that, we've also built a data warehouse for e-com brands that pulls Shopify sales, ad spend across channels, and variable costs into one place so you can see contribution margin at a glance.

Happy to take on paid services too. Martin and I have managed $10M/year e-com brands, so it's familiar ground.

Once you've thought through these, want to jump on 30 min and walk through the actual proposal with numbers?

Juan
