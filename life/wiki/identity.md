## Thinking & Communication Style

**How Juan thinks:**
- **Systems thinker who leads with financial first principles.** Entry point for every problem: what metric are you optimizing for, and is it the right one?
- **Contrarian on conventional metrics.** Signature intellectual move: expose the gap between a popular proxy metric (ROAS, revenue, standard ICE) and actual profit. Then propose a replacement rooted in unit economics.
- **Scientific method applied to marketing.** Frames decisions as experiments with statistical power, confidence levels, and minimum detectable effects. Not intuition-driven.
- **Temporal thinker.** Distrusts static snapshots. Wants cohort behavior over time — 60-day LTV, 120-day LTV, 1-year LTV. A test that looks like a failure at day 0 can be a winner by day 60.
- **Reverse-engineers from profitability.** Instead of "maximize revenue, see what's left," he starts with required profitability and works backward to permissible revenue and ad spend.
- **Wants Claude to push back and think critically, not just agree.**

**How Juan communicates:**
- **Direct, specific, no fluff.** Writes like a financial analyst who became a growth practitioner. No filler phrases, no hedging, no emoji.
- **Numbers as credibility.** Not "shipping costs can be high" but "MercadoLibre's 30% commission." Not "ROAS overstates" but "overestimated by 150-300%." Specificity is a signature.
- **Problem → diagnosis → prescription.** Top-down structure: here's what everyone believes, here's why it's wrong (with math), here's the replacement framework, here's how to implement it.
- **Names his frameworks.** Powered ICE, Marginal Contribution, the Growth Formula. Builds proprietary vocabulary deliberately.
- **Writes from the founder's perspective, not the agency's.** Always frames things as "what does this mean for the owner of this business?"
- **Provocative headlines, rigorous bodies.** "Your ROAS Doesn't Mean Shit" opens the door; the math inside closes it.
- **Values information with dates and sources — skeptical of unverified claims.**
