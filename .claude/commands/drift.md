Find what Juan's subconscious is pulling toward. READ-ONLY.

## Steps

1. Read indexes for both spaces. Note the explicit page topics.
2. Scan wiki pages in full — both `life/wiki/` and `content/wiki/`. Look for:
   - Phrases or concepts that appear in 3+ unrelated pages (pages not already linked via `related:`)
   - Metaphors or framings Juan returns to without labeling as a topic
   - Questions he raises in more than one place without answering
3. Scan the last 60 days of log entries for the same patterns.
4. Discount obvious recurrences (if a word appears everywhere because it's his job, that's not drift — it's baseline).

## Output

- **Drift candidates** — 3-5 recurring ideas that span unrelated notes. For each:
  - The pattern (a phrase, question, or concept)
  - Where it appears (list of pages and log dates)
  - Why it qualifies as drift (not already a named topic, spans unrelated domains)
  - A question: "Is this becoming something?"

- **One sentence** per candidate speculating what it might be maturing into.

## Rules

- Quality over quantity. If there's only one real drift pattern, report one.
- Exclude topics that already have dedicated pages — those aren't drift, they're tracked.
- Cite every appearance.
- Keep under ~400 words.
