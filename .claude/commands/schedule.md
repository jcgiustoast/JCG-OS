Map stated priorities to a suggested weekly time allocation. READ-ONLY.

## Steps

1. Read `life/wiki/professional.md` and `life/wiki/projects.md` for current priorities and commitments.
2. Read the last 14 days of `life/memory/log.md` and `content/memory/log.md` to see how Juan has actually been spending time (based on what the logs say he worked on).
3. JCG-OS has no calendar integration. Ask Juan once: "Any fixed commitments this week I should work around? (meetings, travel, focus blocks)" If he doesn't answer, proceed with assumptions and flag them.

## Output

- **Stated priorities** — 3-5 bullets, pulled from the wiki. Cite pages.
- **Recent time allocation** — what the logs suggest he's actually been working on. One sentence per theme, with % or rough share.
- **Suggested weekly shape** — block-style, e.g.:
  - Mon/Wed/Fri morning — deep work on [priority 1]
  - Tue/Thu — Mars Men synchronous (team, reviews)
  - Fri afternoon — weekly lint + compile
  - Etc.
  Don't pretend to know exact times. Suggest shapes, not clock slots.
- **Conflicts** — places where stated priorities don't match recent allocation. Be direct: "You say X matters, logs show you worked on Y. Which is actually true?"
- **One question** for Juan to decide before the week starts.

## Rules

- This is a proposal, not a calendar. Juan owns the actual scheduling.
- No false precision. If you don't know duration, say "a block" or "half a day."
- Cite every priority claim with `[[page]]` or `log [YYYY-MM-DD]`.
- Keep under ~400 words.
