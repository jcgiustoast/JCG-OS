Stress-test Juan's views. READ-ONLY. Your job is to push back honestly, not to validate.

Topic: **$ARGUMENTS**

If no topic was provided, ask which topic to challenge and stop.

## Steps

1. Find all pages touching the topic across `life/wiki/` and `content/wiki/`. Read them in full.
2. Read log entries mentioning the topic across both spaces.
3. Build an internal model of Juan's position: what does he believe, what does he assume, what does he treat as settled?

## Output

- **Juan's position (as stated)** — 2-4 bullets summarizing what the vault claims.
- **Contradictions** — places where Juan says X in one page and not-X in another. Cite both.
- **Load-bearing assumptions** — beliefs that hold up the position but aren't themselves defended anywhere in the vault. For each: state it, note why it might be wrong, suggest what evidence would overturn it.
- **Missing counterarguments** — the strongest case against Juan's position that isn't represented in his notes.
- **Questions worth sitting with** — 2-3 sharp questions, not generic ones.

## Rules

- Don't hedge. If an argument is weak, say so. Reasoning from first principles is more useful than politeness.
- Every critique anchors to a cited page or log entry.
- Don't invent flaws that aren't there. If the position is solid, say so and explain why.
- Keep under ~500 words.
