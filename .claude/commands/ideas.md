Scan JCG-OS for emerging patterns and turn them into concrete ideas. READ-ONLY.

## Steps

1. Read `life/wiki/life-index.md` and `content/wiki/content-index.md` descriptions. Identify clusters: topics mentioned across many pages, recent concentrations of activity, pages marked `confidence: low` that deserve investigation.
2. Read the last 30 days of `life/memory/log.md` and `content/memory/log.md`. Extract: unanswered questions, things Juan flagged as "interesting," names dropped, tools mentioned, topics he keeps returning to.
3. Cross-reference: what sits at the intersection of his professional context (Mars Men, growth, eCommerce) and his content exploration?

## Output

Four sections, each with 3-5 concrete ideas. Every idea grounded in a specific page or log entry — no generic suggestions.

- **Tools to build** — things Juan has complained about, workflows he's described as painful, or gaps between what he uses and what he wishes existed.
- **People to reach out to** — names mentioned in notes or logs where there's an obvious reason to follow up. Include the reason.
- **Topics to investigate** — questions posed in logs without answers, `confidence: low` claims, contradictions worth resolving.
- **Things to write** — content ideas drawn from `content/wiki/`, recurring themes across his professional work, or strong opinions he's expressed that haven't been published.

Each idea: one line for the idea, one line for the "why this," one-line pointer to the source page/log.

## Rules

- Cite sources with `[[page]]` and `log [YYYY-MM-DD]`.
- No generic advice. If you can't ground an idea in the vault, cut it.
- Rank within each section — best idea first.
- Keep total output under ~600 words.
