description: Identify clusters of ideas ready to become a project, essay, or product
---

Find patterns that have crossed the threshold from "interesting" to "shippable." READ-ONLY.

## Steps

1. Read both `wiki/index.md` files. Identify pages created or updated in the last 60 days.
2. Read `related:` frontmatter across those pages. Build a mental graph of clusters — pages linking to each other densely.
3. Cross-check with log entries (last 60 days) to see which clusters Juan has been actively adding to.
4. Rate each cluster against these filters:
   - **Density**: 4+ pages linked together
   - **Momentum**: recent activity (pages updated or logs mentioning it in last 30 days)
   - **Shape**: has the makings of a concrete output (essay, project, product, talk)

## Output

For each cluster that passes all three filters:

- **Cluster name** — one line, your label.
- **Pages in the cluster** — list with `[[page]]` citations.
- **What it could become** — pick one: essay, project, product, or talk. Explain why that format fits.
- **What's missing** — the one or two gaps before it's ready to ship.
- **First move** — the single next action that turns this cluster into real output.

If no cluster passes, say so. Don't force emergence that isn't there.

## Rules

- 3 clusters max. Prioritize strongest.
- Ground every claim in specific pages.
- Keep under ~500 words.
