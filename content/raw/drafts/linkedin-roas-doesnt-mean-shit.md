Your 3.0 ROAS is a 1.33.

Here's the math most operators never run:

Revenue: $100
Acquisition: -$33
VAT: -$10
Product (30%): -$30
Shipping: -$5
Returns: -$10
Processing: -$5

Real margin: $7.

That's a 1.33 real ROAS. Fixed costs haven't touched it yet.

ROAS has three problems, and every 7-figure operator I talk to is running their business on all three:

1) It's inflated. Meta's default window is 7-day click / 1-day view. Google attributes purchases up to 30 days post-click. Without incrementality testing, reported ROI is overstated by 150-300%.

2) It ignores unit economics. A 3.0 ROAS on a 30% COGS product with 10% returns is breakeven. A 2.0 ROAS on a 15% COGS product is a printing press.

3) It has no time dimension. If new-customer LTV grows 70% by day 90, a breakeven ROAS on day 0 is the correct answer. ROAS can't see that.

The replacement hierarchy:

Total Marginal Contribution -> Total MER -> New vs. recurring split -> Incremental ROAS.

`MARGINAL CONTRIBUTION = Revenue - Variable Costs - Ad Spend`

Start there. Let the channel-level metrics inform, not decide.

What's your Marginal Contribution trend looking like the last 90 days -- and do you know it without opening a spreadsheet?
