# 11 Profitability Levers That 7-Figure eCommerce Businesses Ignore

**Juan Cruz Giusto**
June 25, 2025

_(and they're leaving millions on the table)_

While most obsess over a single metric -- increasing sales -- the most profitable eCommerce businesses see beyond that.

In my experience auditing P&Ls of companies between 1-10M EUR annually, I've identified **11 levers that can unlock between 15-40% additional profitability** without touching the acquisition budget.

---

## 1. Price Testing

90% of the eCommerce businesses I audit have never systematically tested their prices.

Critical error.

**Why do we assume the price we've set is the right one?**

Perhaps if we lower it by 10%, we increase the conversion rate by 20%. Or the opposite: if we increase the price by 15%, we maintain the same conversion rate.

Both scenarios have the same result: more money in your bank account with the same traffic.

With a recent client, an 8% price increase kept conversion intact and added **180K EUR annually directly to EBITDA**.

Understanding your price elasticity through these tests is extraordinarily powerful and can generate a direct impact on your bottom line.

## 2. Implement Bid Limits

If your team doesn't operate with strict CPA or minimum ROAS objectives, you're literally giving away margin to the platforms.

Basically you're telling Facebook and Google: "Get customers, try to do it profitably, but if you can't, it doesn't matter, keep spending."

Facebook and Google optimize for their revenue, not for your profitability. **Their ultimate goal, after all, is for you to spend more and more money**, which is not aligned with yours: earning more profit.

Within the platforms you have the ability to set Bid Controls, whether by CPA or by ROAS.

Make sure the bottleneck isn't in the efficiency of your investment, but in volume.

Growth at any cost is out of fashion.

## 3. Audit and Renegotiate Supplier Contracts

Your sales volume from 18 months ago isn't the same as today, so why don't your supplier contracts reflect that?

If you have prices set two years ago and never renegotiate despite buying more, it's time for a phone call.

I've seen **5-12% reductions in variable costs** (COGS, logistics, software, Amazon and Shopify commissions, etc.) simply by renegotiating terms based on current volumes.

You'd be surprised how many suppliers are willing to negotiate new terms to keep you from leaving.

This tactic can free up tens of thousands in annual gross income.

## 4. Run Incrementality Tests on Advertising

We all know (or intuit) that Facebook and Google "embellish" their results.

Platform-reported ROAS tends to **overestimate real impact by 20-60%**, depending on campaign type.

For example, Google Brand Search campaigns exaggerate their results by up to 5x.

And many businesses act as if nothing is happening, increasing their investment without knowing they're actually losing money.

If you invest more than 10K EUR monthly in digital, incrementality tests are mandatory to understand the real ROI of each channel.

These tests help you understand the real impact of your advertising and prevent you from being fooled by the ROAS the platform shows. This way you understand the real role of each channel, campaign, and strategy.

And we stop giving money to Zuckerberg, who already has enough.

## 5. Optimize the Promoted Product Mix

Not all products in your eCommerce are equal:

* Some have more demand than others
* Acquisition costs vary
* Some products have better gross margin (cheaper to produce or ship)
* Some generate better lifetime value, even if they're cheaper
* Certain products have been sitting in inventory for months without selling, limiting your cash flow

Consider all these aspects when promoting products, both on ad platforms and on your website.

Determine which ones are the most profitable, with the highest demand and that generate solid LTV.

Prioritize promoting products that maximize total contribution and cash flow, not just sales volume.

## 6. Improve Return Rates

Returns are a silent margin killer -- invisible in platform reports but devastating on the real P&L.

**I've worked with brands that have up to 20% return rates.** A 5% reduction in returns typically equals an 8-12% increase in net margin.

If your business is one of them, you need to prioritize this problem:

* Implement questionnaires to understand return reasons
* Study the responses and create an action plan
* Improve product quality, implement AI-powered size guides, or add instruction manuals

The risk of implementing these changes is almost zero and can have a massive impact on your P&L.

## 7. Develop Recurring Revenue Sources

The Holy Grail of eCommerce isn't the first sale, but the ones that follow.

Implementing subscription or membership models can transform your P&L with predictable revenue and reduced acquisition costs.

After all, **you only have to acquire the customer once.**

If your product is recurring in nature, offer subscriptions.

If not, create memberships with exclusive discounts and access to premium content or communities, like True Classic does.

## 8. Automate and Streamline Marketing Operations

This isn't just the finance and operations team's job. It's also marketing's responsibility.

There are thousands of ways to reduce fixed costs within your team. Here are some:

**Efficiency through AI:** With current tools, copy creation, creatives, web pages, and even videos can be largely automated. Invest time in teaching your team how to integrate AI into their daily work and you'll see an immediate productivity increase.

**Tool audit:** You're surely paying for apps you don't use or that add little value. Do a thorough review, determine what truly helps your team, and cancel the rest. Typical savings are between 2-5K EUR monthly in unnecessary subscriptions.

I assure you there are thousands of euros monthly waiting to be unlocked in your OPEX.

## 9. Create Marketing Moments That Generate Demand

Not everything in marketing is generic evergreen ads. It's also about giving the customer a reason to buy today, not tomorrow.

Think about what days and events you can leverage:

* Seasonal trends specific to your vertical
* Relevant industry events
* High purchase-intent moments for your customer base

If you sell children's products: back to school in August-September. If you sell men's products: Movember movement.

**Be creative and give your customer a reason to buy from you today.**

## 10. Diversify Into New Market Segments

Sometimes, generating more creatives and videos isn't enough.

To increase profitability and revenue you need to create products that satisfy new needs and unlock new markets.

**Successful examples:**

* Blue Banana launched their Athletics collection to capture the sports market
* RIDGE constantly releases new wallet colors and develops products like powerbanks, rings, and suitcases

Talk to your customer, research the market, discover unmet needs, and develop products for them.

## 11. Eliminate Destructive Discounts (BONUS)

Constant discounts train customers to wait for promotions and erode brand value.

**Elasticity studies demonstrate** that a large portion of discount-driven sales are non-incremental -- they generate sales that would have occurred anyway.

Byron Sharp has extensively documented this phenomenon in his book How Brands Grow.

Don't abuse them. Use them sporadically to:

* Increase their impact
* Not damage margins
* Protect your brand
