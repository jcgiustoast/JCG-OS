# The Complete eCommerce Metrics Framework: Why Your Growth Has Stalled and How to Fix It

**Author:** Juan Cruz Giusto
**Published:** June 24, 2025

## Introduction

If your eCommerce lacks profitable growth, the issue stems from optimizing the wrong metrics. While focusing on a single metric isn't practical for complex eCommerce systems, success requires understanding the correct hierarchy of metrics and their relationships.

## Level 1: Financial Metrics -- Your True North

Financial metrics represent your business's core reality and ultimate objective: generating positive return on investment.

### Contribution Margin: The Definitive Operating Metric

Contribution margin is calculated as:

**REVENUE - VARIABLE COSTS (COGS + SHIPPING + COMMISSIONS) - ADVERTISING INVESTMENT**

In eCommerce, advertising investment should be treated as a variable cost rather than operating expense, as it typically represents 15-35% of revenue in 7 and 8-figure businesses. Performance marketing scales directly with revenue, making it behave like a true variable cost.

The resulting contribution margin funds three critical areas:

- Coverage of fixed operational costs
- Inventory investment to sustain growth
- Dividend distribution to shareholders

This serves as your operational North Star Metric because teams can control it daily and it reflects financial reality most accurately.

### Profit: Closest to Reality

**Profit = Contribution Margin - Fixed Expenses**

While more complex to measure daily, leaders must monitor profit for medium and long-term strategic decisions. CMOs can directly impact this through team streamlining, AI automation leveraging, and unnecessary cost elimination.

## Level 2: Business Metrics -- Health Indicators

These metrics reveal operational health and strategy effectiveness without excessive tactical detail.

### Revenue

Gross sales minus discounts, representing what platforms like Shopify or WooCommerce report during specific periods.

### Advertising Investment

Total advertising spending aimed at generating direct sales in the channel.

**Critical Multi-Channel Consideration:** When selling across Amazon, physical stores, and brand advertising, prorate costs according to each channel's revenue contribution rather than assigning all investment to your online store.

### MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio)

**Revenue / Advertising Investment**

This indicates revenue generated per euro invested in advertising.

**Warning:** Avoid over-optimizing this metric alone. Pursuing high MER without considering absolute revenue risks under-investing in advertising, limiting long-term growth for short-term efficiency gains.

## Level 3: Customer Metrics -- Protecting Your Future

These metrics ensure sustainable growth and prevent short-term optimization that compromises future performance.

**Key Risk:** Increasing revenue through excessive recurring customer emails advances sales that would occur naturally, depleting future potential.

**Correct Approach:** Prioritize new customer acquisition (future revenue foundation) while maintaining recurring customer engagement through new campaigns and product development.

### Number of New and Recurring Customers

Monitor both absolute numbers and the segment relationship.

### aMER (Acquisition Marketing Efficiency Ratio)

**New Customer Revenue / Advertising Investment**

Since advertising investment primarily targets new customer acquisition (while email and SMS serve recurring customers more economically), this metric reveals true advertising efficiency.

### nCAC (New Customer Acquisition Cost)

**Advertising Investment / Number of New Customers**

Indicates acquisition cost per new customer, whether arriving organically or through paid channels.

### First Order Gross Margin / nCAC

**Critical Note:** Use gross margin (revenue minus variable costs), not average order value.

Ratios below 1 indicate customer acquisition losses. Few eCommerce businesses can sustain this long-term.

**Keep this ratio above 1.**

### AOV of New and Recurring Customers

These metrics reveal product mix promoted across web, social media, advertising, and email/SMS channels.

## Level 4: Platform Metrics -- Directional Indicators

**Critical Warning:** These metrics are furthest from financial reality.

Unlike previous metrics derived from business backend data, platform metrics are indicative rather than absolute. Facebook, Google, TikTok, and attribution platforms like Triple Whale or Northbeam rely on cookies for reporting and lack 100% reliability -- they serve as decision-making proxies.

### ROAS

**Platform-Attributed Revenue / Advertising Spend on Platform**

### iROAS (Incremental ROAS)

When conducting incrementality studies, apply that factor to platform ROAS for more accurate reality reflection.

### In-Platform CAC

**Advertising Spend on Platform / Sales Attributed by Platform**

Remember: These metrics provide directional guidance, not absolute truths.

## The Framework in Action: There's No King Metric

### The Control Paradox

Lower pyramid positions offer greater control and action capacity. While direct contribution margin influence proves difficult, improving Meta's ROAS through new creatives or campaign restructuring is straightforward.

### The Precision Paradox

Higher pyramid positions provide closer financial reality proximity. Reliability decreases moving downward, necessitating simultaneous consideration of all levels to avoid biased decisions optimizing one level at another's expense.

## From Understanding to Execution: The Next Critical Step

Understanding this metrics ecosystem initiates profitable, predictable scaling -- but represents only the beginning.

Real differentiation requires creating:

- Forecasts for each metric
- Specific daily expectations
- Early warning systems for deviations

This approach identifies exactly which metric is failing and prescribes specific corrective actions.

Different problems require different solutions: low revenue from new customers demands different action than low recurring customer performance.
