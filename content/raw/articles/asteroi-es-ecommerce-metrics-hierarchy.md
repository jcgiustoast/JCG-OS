# The Metrics Hierarchy in eCommerce Growth: The Framework Every Executive Needs to Master

**Author:** Juan Cruz Giusto
**Date:** September 26, 2025

## Introduction

If your eCommerce isn't growing profitably, the root cause lies in a single decision: **you're optimizing for the wrong metric**.

Optimizing metrics disconnected from your profitability is the most direct recipe for failure. But focusing on a single metric isn't practical or actionable either for a system as complex as an eCommerce business.

The difference between those who scale sustainably and those who stagnate lies in **understanding the correct hierarchy of metrics** and knowing the relationship between each one.

This analysis presents the complete metrics framework used by 7- and 8-figure eCommerce businesses to grow profitably, allowing you to identify exactly where the bottleneck is when growth stalls and what specific actions to take to fix it.

---

## Level 1: Financial Metrics -- Your True North

Financial metrics represent the purest reality of your eCommerce. They are the closest to your bank account and to the ultimate goal of any business: **generating a positive return on investment**.

### Contribution Margin: The Definitive Operational Metric

For eCommerce purposes, contribution margin is defined as:

> **REVENUE -- VARIABLE COSTS (COGS + SHIPPING + COMMISSIONS) -- AD SPEND**

While in traditional finance ad spend may be classified as operating expense (OPEX), in eCommerce this is a mistake. **Why?** Because this expense represents between 15% and 35% of revenue in 7- and 8-figure eCommerce businesses. To put this in perspective, HIMS -- a telemedicine eCommerce in the USA -- invests more than 40% of its revenue in marketing.

Since most of what's invested goes to Performance Marketing, this expense scales directly with revenue, behaving like a real variable cost.

**The resulting contribution margin funds three critical pillars:**

* Coverage of fixed operating costs
* Inventory investment to sustain growth
* Dividend distribution to shareholders

**This should be your operational North Star Metric** because your team has total control over it daily and it's the metric closest to your financial reality.

### Profit: The Closest to Reality

**Profit = Contribution Margin -- Fixed Expenses**

Although it's more complex to measure daily, it's essential that leaders monitor it for medium- and long-term strategic decisions.

CMOs have direct tools to impact this metric: streamlining teams, leveraging AI for automation, and eliminating unnecessary costs. **Every optimization here translates directly into higher profitability.**

## Level 2: Business Metrics -- Health Indicators

These metrics reveal the general health of your operation and whether your strategy is working, without getting lost in tactical details.

### Revenue

Gross sales minus discounts granted. This is what Shopify or WooCommerce report for a specific period.

### Ad Spend

Total invested in advertising with the direct objective of generating sales in the channel.

**Critical consideration for multi-channel:** If you also sell on Amazon, have a physical store, and invest in brand advertising, you must prorate costs according to the revenue level of each channel. Don't let your online store absorb 100% of the ad spend.

### MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio)

> **Revenue / Ad Spend**

**Indicates how many euros your eCommerce generates for every euro invested in advertising.**

**Critical warning:** Don't over-optimize this metric. If you only seek a high MER without considering absolute revenue, you can fall into the trap of under-investing in advertising, limiting your long-term growth in exchange for short-term efficiency.

## Level 3: Customer Metrics -- Protecting Your Future

These metrics **protect the sustainability** of your eCommerce and ensure you're not optimizing the short term at the expense of the future.

**The real danger:** If you hit revenue targets by sending more emails than you should to recurring customers, you're emptying your piggy bank and bringing forward sales that would have occurred naturally.

**The right approach:** Focus on new customer acquisition, which is the foundation of future revenue, without losing sight of recurring customers (with new campaigns and product development).

### Number of New and Recurring Customers

Monitor both the absolute number and the ratio between both segments.

### aMER (Acquisition Marketing Efficiency Ratio)

> **New Customer Revenue / Ad Spend**

Since most of your ad spend is directed at acquiring new customers (you have cheaper channels for recurring ones: email, SMS, social media), this metric **reveals the true efficiency of your ad spend**.

### nCAC (New Customer Acquisition Cost)

> **Ad Spend / Number of New Customers**

**Tells you how much it costs to acquire a new customer,** regardless of whether they come organically or through paid channels.

### First Order Gross Margin / nCAC

**Note:** This is not First Order AOV. It's the gross margin (sales minus all variable costs) of the first order.

If this ratio is below 1, **you're acquiring customers at a loss**. Very few eCommerce businesses can sustain this.

Keep this number above 1.

### AOV of New and Recurring Customers

These metrics reveal the product mix you promote on your website, social media, advertising, and email/SMS.

## Level 4: Platform Metrics -- Directional Indicators

**Critical warning:** These are the metrics furthest from your financial reality.

Unlike the previous ones (which you get from your business backend), platform metrics are **indicative**, not absolute. Facebook, Google, TikTok, and attribution platforms like Triple Whale or Northbeam depend on cookies to report results.

**They're not 100% reliable. They're proxies for decision-making.**

### ROAS

> **Platform-Attributed Revenue / Platform Ad Spend**

### iROAS (Incremental ROAS)

If you can run incrementality studies, apply that factor to platform ROAS to **more faithfully reflect the reality** of your efforts.

### In-Platform CAC

> **Platform Ad Spend / Platform-Attributed Sales**

**Remember:** These metrics are directional, not absolute truths.

---

## The Framework in Action: There Is No Single King Metric

**The control paradox:** The lower you go in the pyramid, the more control and ability to act you have.

It's difficult to directly influence contribution margin, but it's easy to improve Meta ROAS with new creatives or by restructuring campaigns.

**The precision paradox:** The higher you go in the pyramid, the closer you are to financial reality.

Reliability decreases as you go down levels. **That's why you need to consider all of them simultaneously** to avoid biased decisions that optimize one level at the expense of another.

---

## From Understanding to Execution: The Critical Next Step

Understanding this metrics ecosystem is the first step to scaling profitably and predictably.

**But it's only the first.**

**What really makes the difference is creating:**

* A forecast for each metric
* Specific daily expectations
* Early warning systems for when you're deviating

This allows you to identify exactly **which metric is failing and what specific action to take.**

If revenue is down because of new customers, you'll act differently than if the problem is with recurring customers.
