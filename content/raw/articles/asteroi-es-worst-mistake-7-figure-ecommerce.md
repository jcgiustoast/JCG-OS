> **THE TEAMS ARE OPTIMIZING FOR THE WRONG METRIC.**

If your team doesn't have Marginal Contribution as their objective, you're losing money somewhere.

Let me explain why...

---

## The Problem with Using Revenue as Metric #1

Most eCommerce businesses use revenue as the star metric to measure performance and growth.

But revenue is far removed from what truly matters: profit.

**In the P&L, it is literally the most distant metric from actual profit.**

The result of this blind obsession with revenue?

* We invest in advertising at a loss (hey, we have to hit the target)
* We create promotions that destroy margins and damage the brand
* We send excessive emails/SMS that exhaust the patience of recurring customers
* We celebrate sales "records" while money deteriorates

Target hit, profitability destroyed.

The numbers look spectacular in presentations. The graph is exponential.

But if you look behind it, **they hide a worrying financial reality**.

---

## The Solution: Marginal Contribution as the North Star Metric

**All of this would be solved if we prioritized Marginal Contribution** above all other metrics.

Not buried in the P&L where nobody sees it, but at the top -- visible to the entire team, all the time.

### Definition of Marginal Contribution (in eCommerce)

**NET REVENUE** (Total Revenue - VAT - Returns)

*Important to subtract returns to avoid inflating revenue*

--

**DELIVERY COST** (Product + Shipping + Payment Commission)

*That is, all variable costs necessary for the product to reach the customer.*

--

**ADVERTISING SPEND** (Performance + Brand, prorated by sales channel)

*If we have more sales channels like Amazon or Retail, prorate brand advertising so its full weight doesn't fall on the online store*

=

**MARGINAL CONTRIBUTION**

This money is what actually remains to cover fixed costs, invest in growth, and distribute dividends.

---

## Why Marginal Contribution and Not Another Metric?

It meets two critical requirements that other metrics don't:

* **Proximity to profitability:** It is the closest metric to actual profit
* **Daily measurement:** You can calculate it every day, unlike net profit which requires waiting for the monthly close
* **Control:** The marketing team has direct control over most of its components

---

## How to Implement This Change

Marginal Contribution should be the first metric in your dashboard, and the one that defines all other business variables:

* How much to bill
* How much to invest in advertising
* Maximum acceptable CAC
* Timing of promotions

**And the second key is the forecast.**

Always start with your Marginal Contribution target and reverse-engineer how much you need to bill, how much you can invest in ads, and which margins are non-negotiable.

This approach completely inverts traditional logic.

Instead of chasing revenue at any cost, you first define how much profitability you need and work backward.

> "What is not measured is not managed." -- Peter Drucker

**And when your team sees and pursues this metric every day, decisions change automatically:**

- Advertising spend only with demonstrated positive return
- Extreme care with discounts that damage margins
- Strategies focused on profitability, not short-term revenue
- Inventory decisions based on real profitability

**The result?** Sustainable growth that truly generates value and protects the future of the business.
