# What Is Meta Andromeda and 7 Strategies for Your eCommerce to Get the Most Out of It

**Author:** Juan Cruz Giusto
**Date:** October 21, 2025

---

## Introduction

With the launch of Meta Andromeda, the rules of the game in eCommerce have changed. If you continue using 2024 strategies, you are falling behind.

This guide covers:
- What Meta Andromeda is
- What has changed and what remains the same
- How your eCommerce can make the most of this algorithm shift

---

## What Is Andromeda: Meta Ads' New Algorithm

Andromeda is a new Meta Ads algorithm introduced at the end of 2024, fully implemented by October 2025.

The little-known reality is that **Andromeda is just 1 of 4 new tools launched:**

### 1. Meta GEM: The Brain

Generative Ads Recommendation Model allows the algorithm to process millions of creatives quickly to show personalized, relevant ads to users.

### 2. Meta Lattice: The Library

A new architecture for ranking ads that allows Meta to learn across multiple campaigns and ad sets, rather than limiting learning to a single ad set.

### 3. Meta Andromeda: The Worker

Finds the perfect ad for each user among tens of millions available, considering hundreds of variables: location, day, previously viewed ads, behavior inside and outside of Meta.

### 4. Sequence Learning: The Memory

An AI model that considers the actions a person takes before and after seeing your ad, delivering the right creative. Example: if someone buys a hotel ticket for skiing, they will see ads about ski rental.

### Why Such a Drastic Change

The reason is simple: **AI-generated creatives**.

Before, teams could create dozens of images and videos monthly. With AI, they now generate hundreds in weeks, plus variations created by Meta itself.

Meta's old algorithm could not handle and analyze this new volume. Andromeda solves this problem.

## 7+1 Strategies to Get the Most Out of Andromeda

### 1. Creative Volume

With Andromeda, the algorithm can efficiently process up to **50 creatives per ad set** (previously: 5-10).

To win in this landscape: **exponentially increase the number of creatives**.

If you were creating 5 per week, generate 20 or 50.

Leading companies like Loop Earplugs, Gruns, and Ridge have up to 1,000 active ads and generate more than 200 different creatives monthly.

**The number of creatives is directly proportional to your eCommerce's success.**

### 2. Creative Diversity

This does not mean generating iterations of the same image with color or text changes. Meta detects when a creative is merely an iteration and reduces its impact.

What matters is not just volume, but **diversity in your ads**.

Meta states: "the focus has shifted to creative diversification as the best means to find relevant audiences."

Generate different creatives across multiple dimensions:
- By awareness level
- By format and placement
- By buyer persona
- By angle
- By product

Create a framework to systematically test which combinations work.

### 3. Broad Targeting

Meta has exponentially increased its capacity to process user and creative data.

**If you target by interests and lookalikes, you are obstructing the algorithm**.

Keep your audience as broad as possible, configuring only gender, age, and geographic location. Meta will find who responds best to your creatives.

"The creative is the targeting, not your manual configuration."

Strategy by objective:
- **Abandoned cart:** Ads about discounts
- **New audiences:** Talk about the problem, not the product

### 4. Consolidation of Campaigns and Ad Sets

Meta recommends **minimum 20 creatives, maximum 50 per ad set**.

The algorithm needs volume and diversity to function optimally.

Separate campaigns only by cost per result and products. If the ads talk about the same product and approach, they go in the same ad set. This reduces the learning phase.

### 5. AI Creatives

Meta reports: "campaigns using image generation in Advantage+ creative experience 11% higher click-through rates and 7.6% more conversions."

Although it sometimes looks strange or off-brand (weird music, changed colors), **Meta assures it works better and lowers acquisition costs**.

Coming soon: generating images from a product photo.

### 6. Post-Click Experience

With Sequence Learning, Meta considers what happens on your website to rank ads.

It is now crucial to improve:
1. Landing page
2. Overall purchase process
3. Post-purchase experience

Without an optimized flow from ad to thank-you page, your ads will eventually fail because **Meta will factor in that poor experience**.

Invest in CRO and customer service.

### 7. Less Manual Interaction

Common mistake: seeing a high CPA after two days, reducing budget or turning off creatives with low ROAS.

**Trust the algorithm, let it optimize, do not act prematurely**.

Meta has millions of data points to determine whether a creative will perform well now and in the future. Your judgment does not outperform the algorithm.

Good past performance does not guarantee future results, especially with elevated budgets (phenomenon: "The Breakdown Effect").

It is better to let Andromeda optimize while you focus on strategy.

### 8. Data Quality

Nothing works if you are not tracking data correctly.

**Andromeda is AI and machine learning using data as fuel**. If data is unreliable, your advertising suffers.

Use CAPI and server-side tracking (Shopify natively or Elevar with budget).

Ensure that the Event Match Quality Score on important events (View Product, Add to Cart, Checkout, Purchase) is **greater than 8**.

Otherwise, the algorithm optimizes with unreliable data.

## What Does Not Change with the Introduction of Andromeda

### Spend Controls (Bid Limits)

**It is essential to protect profitability**.

Spend controls or bid limits allow you to tell Meta not to spend if it will not reach a certain CPA or ROAS.

Without them, Meta optimizes for conversion volume at the lowest cost, frequently exceeding your willingness to pay.

This configuration protects your contribution margin and ensures you only invest when Meta is confident it can hit your goals.

### Exclusion of Recent Buyers

Although Meta recommends trusting Advantage Plus campaigns and suggested audiences 100%, it is a mistake not to exclude recent buyers from prospecting campaigns.

Meta prioritizes people who are cheap to convert. **You would be spending on those who would have bought anyway** or were acquired for free via email, SMS, or social media.

Your campaigns will show "worse results" in Ads Manager, but will generate real incremental sales.

### Incrementality Tests

Meta never truly knows the real impact of marketing campaigns.

**If you spend more than 30,000 euros per month, you need incrementality tests.**

Talk to your Meta representative about this.

Without sufficient spend, configure attribution with "7-Day Click," the perfect combination of conversion volume and reporting accuracy.

## Think Beyond Your Ad Account

The real growth lever **is not in Meta**.

What scales your eCommerce 2x, 5x, or 10x is outside:

- New product development
- International expansion
- Stories that truly reach people
- Strategy that builds defensibility against competition
- Realistic forecasts that integrate the team
- etc.

Advertising is complementary, not the main focus.

**True growth happens outside of Ads Manager.**
