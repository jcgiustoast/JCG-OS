# From Losses to Profits: How to Turn Meta Into Your Profitability Machine

**Author:** Juan Cruz Giusto
**Date:** July 16, 2025

## The True Goal of Advertising Platforms

Advertising platforms like Meta prioritize their own profits over those of advertisers. Their central objective is to increase their revenue, not to guarantee the profitability of the businesses investing in them. Although they seemingly seek to optimize results, they actually manipulate metrics so that advertisers spend more money believing they are getting better performance.

## 3 Ways Meta Manipulates Your Results

### 1. They Inflate ROAS Without You Noticing

Meta uses default attribution settings (7-day click and 1-day view) that allow it to claim credit for sales that would have occurred without advertising. According to shared data, up to 50% of purchases attributed to Meta would happen regardless. This makes ROAS appear higher than it actually is.

### 2. The Default Bidding Strategy

The default "Highest Volume or Value" strategy optimizes for volume without guaranteeing profitability. Although it seeks the lowest possible cost, many campaigns generate losses because there are no limits on what can be spent per result. Meta will continue spending even when the cost exceeds the business's ability to remain profitable.

### 3. They Make It Difficult to Exclude Recent Buyers

Meta discourages excluding customers who recently purchased, as this improves their cost-per-acquisition metrics. However, these customers could be reached for free or nearly free through email, social media, or other direct channels.

## Tool 1: Incrementality Tests

Incrementality tests measure the true impact of advertising through controlled experiments. The methodology consists of:

- Selecting similar geographic regions
- Reducing, pausing, or increasing ad spend in some regions
- Comparing revenue changes between treated and control regions
- Evaluating which sales were truly generated by advertising

### Practical Example

You invest EUR 40,000 in two similar cities (EUR 20,000 each): Madrid and Barcelona. Both generate EUR 100,000 in monthly revenue. Meta reports EUR 50,000 in revenue per city (ROAS of 2.5).

When you pause Madrid for a month, its revenue drops to EUR 60,000, while Barcelona maintains EUR 100,000. This indicates that Meta actually generated EUR 40,000, not EUR 50,000. The real ROAS is 2, not 2.5.

### Incrementality Factor

**Formula:** Actual Test Revenue / Platform-Reported Revenue

In the example: 40,000 / 50,000 = 0.8 (80%)

This means Meta's numbers should be reduced by 20% to reflect the real over-attribution. Running these tests regularly allows you to adjust strategies with real data.

## Tool 2: Bid Limits

Meta and Google offer alternative bidding strategies that protect profitability. These only spend budget if the specified cost per result is achieved.

### Target CPA Calculation

For a EUR 100 product:
- 21% VAT
- 25% Variable Costs
- 30% Target Profit

**Maximum allowable CPA: EUR 24**

### The 3 Bidding Strategies

1. **Cost Per Result Goal:** Pays a maximum of X euros per conversion on average. Meta bids above and below but keeps the average under the limit.

2. **ROAS Goal:** For every euro spent, recovers a minimum of X euros. Example: 4:1 ROAS means EUR 4 return for every EUR 1 invested.

3. **Bid Cap:** Pays a maximum of X euros per conversion without exceeding. Meta only bids below the target.

All these strategies ensure that Meta only invests when it can do so profitably.

---

## Bringing It All Together: The Complete Formula

Combining both tools:

**Final calculation:** Cost Target x Incrementality Factor

If the target CPA is EUR 24 and the Incrementality Factor is 0.8:

24 x 0.8 = **EUR 19.20**

When you see a CPA of EUR 19.20 in a campaign, you will know the real cost is EUR 24, guaranteeing profitability.

## Action Plan

1. Request incrementality tests from your Meta representative (if spend justifies it)
2. Calculate and apply the Incrementality Factor to your CPAs
3. Review Ad Manager to identify unprofitable campaigns
4. Pause campaigns that generate losses
5. Reconfigure with alternative bidding strategies that protect margins

The bottleneck will then be creative quality and website quality, not the budget invested.
