# The Formula You Need to Unlock Your eCom Growth

**Author:** Juan Cruz Giusto
**Date:** June 19, 2025

## Core Growth Formula

The foundational equation for scaling eCommerce businesses:

```
GROWTH = (TRAFFIC x CONVERSION RATE x AOV x 60-DAY LTV MULTIPLIER)
         - VARIABLE COSTS - FIXED COSTS
```

The article emphasizes that success requires moving beyond brute-force tactics. Rather than simply doubling ad spend, effective growth demands experimentation across each variable since strategies that work for one business may fail for another.

## Traffic

Traffic represents a critical component, though not the complete story. Three key factors merit attention:

- **CPM:** Cost per thousand impressions
- **CTR:** Click-through rate of ads
- **Incrementality:** True marketing impact beyond platform reporting

Creative assets drive the biggest lever -- outstanding videos, images, or copy can reduce costs and increase clicks. Businesses should investigate:

- Which audience segments respond most favorably
- Which sales angles generate superior results
- Whether images or videos deliver better cost-effectiveness

## Conversion Rate

"Huge amounts of traffic are of no use if they don't end up buying." Computing conversion rate involves dividing total buyers by total users, though comparisons with industry benchmarks can mislead since rates vary significantly across sectors.

### Key Improvement Areas:

**Funnel Conversion Rate**
Track performance from session start through purchase, identifying where users drop off most frequently.

**Ad-Landing Page Alignment**
Inconsistencies between advertisements and destination pages undermine conversion. Elements like messaging, visuals, and color schemes should align seamlessly.

**Segment Analysis**
Analyze underperforming customer groups -- whether by device type, geography, or demographics -- to uncover optimization opportunities.

## Average Order Value (AOV)

This metric indicates the average spending per customer transaction. "A 10% increase in AOV has the same effect as the same increase in Conversion Rate," yet receives less attention from optimization professionals.

Higher AOV enables increased advertising budgets, as businesses capable of paying more for customers gain competitive advantages. Experimental levers include:

- Pricing adjustments
- Upsell and cross-sell tactics
- Shipping threshold modifications

## 60-Day LTV Multiplier

While SaaS commonly targets 3:1 LTV-to-CAC ratios, this approach would destabilize many eCommerce operations. The 60-day measurement represents a practical middle ground, reflecting average customer spending within the first two months post-purchase.

**Example:** Customers acquired in August 2024 with $50 AOV who reach $75 average LTV by October demonstrate a 50% multiplier.

Growth opportunities encompass:

- Subscription offerings
- Post-purchase flow optimization
- Engagement campaigns at 30-45 days post-purchase
- New communication channels (apps, SMS)
- Membership or affiliate programs

Different business types naturally achieve varying multipliers -- coffee brands typically outperform jewelry retailers -- yet improvement remains possible across all sectors.

## Variable Costs

This category includes:

- Cost of goods sold
- Payment processing fees
- Shipping expenses
- Returns

A "2% decrease in variable costs has the same effect as a 2% increase in ARPU," yet this lever often receives overlooked attention. The article cites Hexclad's experiment: reducing bundle contents from 13 to 12 pieces while maintaining price increased gross margins without affecting ARPU.

Additional opportunities:

- Enhanced sizing guides reducing return rates
- Evaluation of payment method profitability
- Supplier negotiations

## Fixed Costs

Non-variable expenses include:

- Customer service
- Rent
- Marketing personnel
- Marketing software

While revenue growth naturally improves fixed cost percentages, experimentation offers alternatives:

- Testing AI support versus human agents for efficiency
- FAQ improvements potentially reducing support tickets
- Verification that peripheral apps (affiliate, SMS) generate genuine incremental revenue
