# The Formula You Need to Unlock Your eCom Growth

**Author:** JC Giusto
**Published:** November 18, 2024

## Core Growth Formula

**GROWTH = (TRAFFIC x CONVERSION RATE x AOV x 60-DAY LTV MULTIPLIER) - VARIABLE COSTS - FIXED COSTS**

According to the author, this equation represents the foundational framework for scaling eCommerce businesses profitably through strategic experimentation.

## Traffic

The first variable encompasses visitor acquisition costs and performance:

- **CPM (Cost Per Mille):** Measures expense per thousand impressions
- **CTR (Click-Through Rate):** Tracks ad engagement rates
- **Incrementality:** Evaluates authentic marketing impact beyond platform attribution

Key optimization areas include creative quality (videos, images, copy), audience targeting, and sales messaging angles. The author emphasizes understanding which approaches deliver superior cost-to-result ratios.

## Conversion Rate

This metric divides total buyers by total users. However, industry-specific benchmarking varies significantly.

**Critical optimization areas:**

- **Funnel Analysis:** Identifying dropout stages between session initiation and purchase
- **Ad-Landing Page Consistency:** Ensuring visual and messaging alignment
- **Segment Performance:** Detecting underperforming demographics or channels

The author notes that addressing mobile performance gaps or geographic conversion disparities creates tangible improvement opportunities.

## Average Order Value (AOV)

This represents "how much, on average, a customer spends each time they place an order in your store." The author argues AOV improvement equals conversion rate improvements in impact.

Optimization strategies include:
- Pricing adjustments
- Upsell and cross-sell tactics
- Shipping threshold modifications

Higher AOV enables increased marketing spend capacity, creating competitive advantages in customer acquisition.

## 60-Day LTV Multiplier

This metric tracks customer spending within 60 days post-purchase, balancing SaaS LTV:CAC ratios with eCommerce economics.

Example provided: A cohort acquired in August 2024 with $50 AOV reaching $75 average LTV by October generates a 50% multiplier.

**Enhancement approaches:**
- Subscription offerings
- Post-purchase flow optimization
- Communication expansion (apps, SMS)
- Membership or affiliate programs

Different industries exhibit naturally varying LTV multipliers (coffee versus jewelry), yet improvement remains achievable across sectors.

## Variable Costs

These scale proportionally with revenue:
- Cost of Goods Sold
- Payment processing fees
- Shipping expenses
- Return costs

The author highlights this often-overlooked lever: "A 2% decrease in variable costs has the same effect as a 2% increase in ARPU." Example cited includes HexClad reducing bundle cookware from 13 to 12 units without price reduction, maintaining ARPU while improving gross margins.

## Fixed Costs

Non-proportional expenses including customer service, rent, personnel, and marketing software.

Experimentation opportunities include:
- A/B testing AI versus human support
- FAQ improvement reducing support tickets
- Evaluating incremental revenue from affiliate or SMS platforms

## Conclusion

The author positions this framework as starting methodology requiring adaptation for individual businesses. The underlying principle emphasizes applying scientific methodology to validate solutions across all business dimensions, extending beyond traditional conversion rate optimization into holistic growth strategy.
