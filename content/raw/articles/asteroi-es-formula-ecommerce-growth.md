# The Formula You Need to Unlock Your eCommerce Growth

**Author:** Juan Cruz Giusto
**Date:** July 30, 2025

---

## Introduction

I've studied hundreds of the best eCommerce businesses. I've worked with dozens generating millions in revenue every year. And this is the formula I found to scale them quickly and profitably.

### The Growth Formula

```
GROWTH = (TRAFFIC * CONVERSION RATE * AOV * 60-DAY LTV MULTIPLIER)
         - VARIABLE COSTS - FIXED COSTS
```

As growth specialists, founders, and CEOs, we can improve each of these variables. Not just through brute force -- like increasing traffic by doubling Facebook spend -- but also by experimenting our way to success.

After running hundreds of experiments, I've realized that **there's no one-size-fits-all strategy**. What increases AOV in one eCommerce business may decrease it in another. And a tactic that improves conversion rate in one may destroy it in another.

That's why **experimentation is key**. This way we can be sure that the changes we implement have a positive impact.

---

## Traffic

Part of any eCommerce business's success depends on the number of visitors it receives. Some say traffic is a vanity metric. In some ways, I agree. But I also believe that **without traffic, your business is dead**.

So why not keep an eye on it without letting this metric drive your strategy? Besides, you're paying for half of it, so it's something you should consider.

### Three Key Factors

1. **CPM:** Measures cost per thousand impressions.
2. **CTR:** Measures the click-through rate of your ad. Multiplied by CPM, you'll get CPC, or Cost Per Click.
3. **Incrementality:** Measures the real impact of your marketing, regardless of what the ad platform reports.

The biggest lever in traffic is your creative. An outstanding video, image, or ad copy can reduce CPMs and increase CTRs, resulting in a lower CPC.

### Key Questions to Consider

- What has a better cost-to-result ratio, images or videos?
- Do you know which audience resonates most with your product?
- Which sales angle produces the best results?

## Conversion Rate

**Large amounts of traffic are useless if they don't end up buying**. That's why conversion rate is a crucial metric in our eCommerce Growth Formula.

Calculating it is easy: divide Total Buyers by Total Users.

But be careful, don't make the mistake of comparing yours with benchmarks you see on the web. Every industry and business is different, and conversion rates vary greatly between them.

### Funnel Conversion Rate

Start tracking funnel step performance, from Session Start to Purchase. You need to identify **at which stages most users drop off** and identify why it's happening. There may be problems at checkout, or lack of motivation on the product page.

### Ad-to-Landing Connection

Inconsistencies between the ad and the landing page kill your conversion rate. Make sure everything a person sees in the ad -- words, images, colors, angles -- is replicated on the landing page. This way, the person has the assurance they're in the right place and can continue their journey toward purchase.

### Segment Analysis

Run reports to detect which segments are converting poorly compared to others. Perhaps mobile is performing worse than desktop, so there's an improvement opportunity. Or maybe your Canadian customers have a worse conversion rate than those in the United States.

Your job is to identify which ones are underperforming, detect why, and work to improve it.

## AOV (Average Order Value)

AOV -- Average Order Value -- **indicates how much a customer spends on average each time they place an order in your store**. Although most CRO practitioners ignore this metric, it's extremely important.

All else being equal, a 10% increase in AOV has the same effect as the same increase in Conversion Rate. And sometimes, it's much easier to improve the former with an upsell and cross-sell strategy.

Improving AOV allows you to bid more for your customers and increase your ad spend. At the end of the day, **the business that can pay more for the customer wins**.

### Strategies to Experiment With

- Pricing
- Upsells and Cross-sells
- Free Shipping Threshold

## 60-Day LTV Multiplier

In SaaS, targeting a 3:1 LTV to CAC ratio is common practice, but this same concept would bankrupt many eCommerce businesses. But completely ignoring LTV is also a mistake. We can unlock enormous growth by including this factor in our strategy. That's why **60-day LTV is the sweet spot between both views**.

This metric reflects how much a customer spends on average in the first 60 days after purchase.

### Example

- We acquire a cohort of users in August 2024 and their AOV is $50
- By October their average LTV is $75
- So our 60-Day LTV Multiplier is 50%

Depending on your business, you may also want to track 90-day, 120-day, 1-year, and 2-year LTV, but 60 days is a good starting point. It's also the easiest to measure and improve since users are more receptive to changes.

### Elements to Experiment With

- Offering subscriptions
- Optimizing your post-purchase flows
- Creating new flows after 30 to 45 days
- Introducing new forms of communication like Apps, SMS, etc.
- Developing membership or affiliate programs

**Some businesses will naturally have a higher LTV Multiplier than others**. Think of brands selling coffee versus jewelry. But that doesn't mean we shouldn't strive to improve it. I've seen major improvements across different industries, so let's get the experiments running.

## Variable Costs

This component includes every cost that increases proportionally with your sales:

- Cost of Goods Sold
- Payment processing fees
- Shipping
- Returns

As Growth and Experimentation specialists, we tend to think about how we can increase our revenue. **But we're forgetting the other side of the equation -- reducing costs**.

A 2% decrease in variable costs has the same effect as a 2% increase in ARPU. And although it seems like there's no room for experimentation, it's a huge and often overlooked lever.

### Example: HexClad

HexClad tested reducing the number of cookware pieces sold in their bundle from 13 to 12 without changing the price. **ARPU didn't drop but gross margin increased due to decreased COGS**.

If you detect that returns are a major cost in your P&L, an improved size guide could reduce it. Or if you see that Afterpay is eating into a percentage of your profits, you could try removing it and verify whether ARPU decreases. Don't sleep on variable costs -- there's a huge opportunity here.

---

## Fixed Costs

These are all costs that don't increase when your sales increase -- at least not proportionally. For example:

- Customer service
- Rent
- Marketing staff
- Marketing apps
- etc.

One way to reduce the percentage these costs represent in your P&L is to increase revenue. But often, this is hard to do. Another option is to think outside the box and **build experiments that aim to reduce them**.

### Proposed Experiments

- **A/B test offering AI support** instead of humans and verify efficiency and quality
- **Improve your FAQs** and determine if that decreases the number of tickets created
- **Investigate whether your affiliate or SMS apps generate incremental revenue or not.** If they don't, eliminate them

There are dozens more ways to experiment with fixed costs. Review your brand's P&L and design tests aimed at reducing them.

## Unlock Your eCommerce Business Growth with Experimentation

This formula is a starting point and doesn't consider variables like inventory or cash flow. But **it's a great way to challenge our understanding of experimentation in eCommerce and expand its scope**.

Because this isn't just about changing button colors and improving conversion rates. It's a way to think through problems and apply the scientific method to validate solutions.

And if you learn to think and act like a scientist, you'll be closer to unlocking the true potential of your business.
