# 7 Habits of Highly Profitable eCom Brands

**Author:** Juan Cruz Giusto
**Date:** June 30, 2025

## Introduction

The eCommerce landscape has fundamentally shifted. "Growth at any cost is dead. Those who continue chasing ROAS end up, sooner or later, hitting the wall."

For brands generating seven to eight figures in revenue, success depends on sustainable profitability rather than vanity metrics. The piece identifies seven distinct practices that distinguish profitable operations from struggling ones.

## The Seven Habits

### 1. Profitability is the North Star, Not Revenue

The foundational principle involves measuring success by profit generation rather than sales volume. Every decision -- from campaign launches to resource allocation -- gets evaluated based on marginal contribution impact and net profit rather than gross revenue or platform metrics.

### 2. Absolute Control of Bidding and Advertising Efficiency

Successful brands maintain strict bidding discipline, with defined minimum CPA or ROAS targets for each campaign. The core insight: "Advertising platforms optimize for their revenue, not for your profit." Without clear limits, margins erode financing competitor growth instead.

### 3. They Test Incrementality, Not Platform ROAS

Platform-reported ROAS can overestimate actual impact. High-performing eCommerces conduct systematic incrementality testing to identify which channels generate genuine additional sales versus those simply attributing conversions that would occur anyway.

### 4. They Analyze and Optimize Their Product Mix

Profitable brands continuously evaluate which products deliver superior margins, lower acquisition costs, and stronger lifetime value. This analysis informs both advertising priorities and website merchandising strategies.

### 5. They Don't Abuse Discounts

Strategic discount deployment replaces indiscriminate promotional activity. Rather than training customers to await sales, successful brands create compelling marketing moments that encourage immediate purchase without sacrificing margins or brand positioning.

### 6. Radical Efficiency in Fixed Costs and Operations

Profitability increases through operational optimization alongside revenue growth. This includes auditing software subscriptions, renegotiating contracts, and implementing AI-powered automation -- potentially freeing 10-20% additional margin.

### 7. Daily Tracking and Unified Dashboard of Marginal Contribution

Real-time dashboards tracking daily marginal contribution replace month-end surprise assessments. This enables immediate course corrections and data-driven decision-making throughout each month.

## Conclusion

Sustainable scaling requires obsession with profitability, operational efficiency, and decisions grounded in authentic data rather than revenue illusions. Businesses implementing these habits transition from volatile growth cycles to predictable profit generation.
