# The Worst Mistake That 7-Figure eCommerces Make

**Author:** Juan Cruz Giusto
**Date:** June 30, 2025

## Opening Scenario

The article begins with a common tension in growing eCommerces:

- **CMO:** Revenue target achieved at 110%
- **Marketing Agency:** Achieved 7x ROAS
- **CFO:** Profitability dropped 20%

## Core Problem: Wrong Metric Optimization

The fundamental issue: "teams are optimizing for the wrong metric." Teams lack Marginal Contribution as their objective, causing money loss.

## Why Revenue Falls Short

Revenue sits far from actual profit in the P&L structure. Obsessing over revenue leads to:

- Unprofitable advertising investments
- Margin-destroying promotions
- Customer fatigue from excessive contact
- Celebrating sales records while finances deteriorate

Spectacularly presented metrics hide concerning financial realities.

## The Solution: Marginal Contribution as North Star

Make Marginal Contribution visible to the entire team constantly -- not buried in financial statements.

### Formula

**Marginal Contribution = NET REVENUE - DELIVERY COST - ADVERTISING INVESTMENT**

Where:

- **Net Revenue:** Total Revenue minus VAT and Returns
- **Delivery Cost:** Product, Shipping, Payment Commission (all variable costs)
- **Advertising Investment:** Performance and brand advertising, prorated across sales channels

This remainder covers fixed expenses, growth investment, and dividend distribution.

## Why Marginal Contribution Beats Alternatives

Two critical advantages:

1. **Proximity to profitability:** Closest metric to actual profit
2. **Daily measurement:** Calculable daily (unlike monthly net profit)
3. **Control:** Marketing teams directly influence most components

## Implementation Strategy

Place Marginal Contribution first on dashboards, defining:

- Billing amounts
- Advertising investment levels
- Maximum acceptable Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Promotion timing

### Reverse Engineering Approach

Start with Marginal Contribution objectives and work backward to calculate necessary revenue, ad spend, and non-negotiable margins. This inverts traditional revenue-chasing logic.

As Peter Drucker noted: "What gets measured gets managed."

## Behavioral Results

When teams track this metric daily, decisions shift:

- Advertising spending only with proven positive returns
- Careful margin protection in discounting
- Profitability-focused strategies over short-term revenue
- Inventory decisions based on real profitability

**Outcome:** Sustainable growth generating genuine value and protecting business futures.
