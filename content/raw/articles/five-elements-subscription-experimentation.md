# 5 Critical Elements for Subscription eCommerce Growth & Experimentation

**By JC Giusto | March 7, 2025**

## Introduction

The author previously published a comprehensive guide on running successful eCommerce growth programs, but after implementing these across multiple subscription brands, has identified five essential differentiators for market leaders.

## 1. Defining the Right North Star Metric

A North Star Metric maintains alignment across the organization regarding what creates customer value and business growth. Traditional eCommerce might use simple metrics like customer count or revenue, but subscription models require more sophistication.

Simply tracking subscriber numbers overlooks a critical gap: "The number of subscribers might seem obvious, but it completely neglects non-subscribers" who represent substantial revenue portions.

An effective North Star must encompass:
- New and returning customers
- Subscribers and one-time buyers
- Average Order Value (AOV)
- Lifetime Value (LTV)
- Profit margins

Anything less risks ignoring crucial business components and misdirecting strategy.

## 2. Robust A/B Testing with Advanced Analytics Integration

Standard eCommerce A/B testing tools typically handle analytics internally, but subscription businesses face unique challenges. When experiments increase subscription signups (often discounted), AOV typically decreases, causing testing tools to incorrectly flag variants as failures.

"To determine the real winner, you need sophisticated LTV analysis -- something most testing tools don't provide" natively. Teams must develop custom solutions using Google Sheets or Looker Studio to incorporate longer-term customer value into decision-making.

## 3. Deep Understanding of Your Business Economics

Effective subscription experiments demand comprehensive knowledge including:

- Average LTV comparing subscribers versus non-subscribers
- LTV progression across timeframes (1-month, 3-month, 6-month, 1-year)
- Gross margins by product type and purchase model
- Subscription customer breakeven points
- Product retention and margin performance comparisons

This granular understanding enables teams to "craft strategies that genuinely unlock revenue and margins rather than superficial metrics" that appear impressive but lack sustainability.

## 4. Statistical Expertise Beyond Basics

Statistics in subscription testing presents unique complexity. The author emphasizes that "Someone on your subscription team needs deep statistical knowledge" to avoid costly misinterpretations.

Subscription timelines differ fundamentally from one-time purchase businesses. Tests appearing unsuccessful today may deliver significant returns within 60 days when subscription renewals commence. Without proper cohort analysis and multi-horizon metric interpretation capabilities, businesses essentially gamble with their future.

## 5. Full-Funnel Experimentation Beyond CRO

The traditional "CRO" terminology narrows focus excessively, particularly for subscription models. Rather than concentrating solely on acquisition metrics, businesses should adopt SaaS-like thinking across multiple levers:

- **Acquisition**: Including AOV and initial subscription take rates
- **Activation**: Optimizing the first 90 days and habit formation
- **Retention**: Building communities and resources promoting subscriber loyalty
- **Revenue**: Emphasizing margins, purchase frequency, and consumption patterns
- **Referral**: Creating systems enabling loyal customers to recruit new ones

## The Scientific Method: Your Path Forward

Intuition serves early-stage businesses, but maturity demands systematic approaches. "Once your subscription business matures beyond $5M in revenue, you need to shift your approach and embrace the scientific method."

The scientific method enables teams to:

1. Understand actual customer behaviors versus stated preferences
2. Implement evidence-supported changes
3. Build sustainable growth on proven results

The alternative involves pursuing ineffective initiatives while potential revenue opportunities disappear.
