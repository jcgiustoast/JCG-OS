# Unlock Your P&L's Growth with Experimentation

**Author:** JC Giusto
**Date:** March 11, 2025

## Overview

The article challenges conventional thinking about conversion rate optimization (CRO) and experimentation, arguing that meaningful growth opportunities often lie beyond website optimization in financial statements rather than user interface tweaks.

## Main Argument

"The real bottleneck isn't on your website, but off of it." The author contends that while UX improvements matter, businesses frequently overlook P&L-based experimentation opportunities that could yield substantially greater returns.

## Variable Costs Experimentation

### Payment Processors

Test alternative payment methods and fee structures. A Speero case study found switching processors reduced conversions by 3.86%, demonstrating the importance of testing financial changes.

### Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

- Test different price points
- Reduce product quantity without price changes
- Experiment with alternative discount structures
- Test cashback versus direct discounts

HexClad reduced their premium cookware set from 13 to 12 pieces without price adjustments, maintaining conversion while increasing gross margins.

### Returns Management

Tradinn decreased size-related returns by 28% through improved sizing guidance. Test options include store credit alternatives, exchange incentives, and sizing tool optimization.

### Shipping Costs

- Experiment with free shipping thresholds (in $5-10 increments)
- Test presentation methods throughout customer journey
- Incentivize local pickup options

### Marketing Optimization

Jones Road Beauty's incrementality testing revealed YouTube ads drove 1.82X more orders than click attribution showed, justifying a spend increase that generated 2.26X more new customer orders.

## Fixed Costs Experimentation

### Customer Service

Organic Basics implemented AI support, achieving 41% ticket automation with 69% resolution rates within four months.

### Marketing Operations

Test AI creative tools versus traditional methods for production efficiency.

### Workplace Models

Stanford research found remote work increased productivity by 13% across 16,000 workers over nine months.

## Implementation Framework

1. Analyze P&L with finance team
2. Prioritize using ICE framework (Impact, Confidence, Ease)
3. Design controlled experiments
4. Implement validated results

The article concludes that while "button colors might move conversion by 2%, optimizing your COGS percentage" through testing unlocks substantially greater growth potential.
