Your eCommerce is like a tree.

And understanding this analogy will help you scale profitably. Three parts:

**The roots: returning customers.**

The most profitable of all — you don't have to reacquire them. They're also the easiest to forecast, because they tend to behave similarly over time.

**The trunk: new organic customers.**

People who already know your brand but haven't bought yet. They follow you on social, they're on your email/SMS list, or they've engaged with your posts.

In other words: the people who would buy from you even without paid media.

**The branches: new paid customers.**

People who don't know you yet. You have to go out and convince them. They're the most expensive to acquire and the hardest to forecast — you depend on external platforms to reach them.

Turn off paid media and these people never buy from you.

**Why this analogy matters.**

Beyond understanding the parts, it helps you think about risk.

A tree without deep roots and with a thin trunk falls the first time a strong wind hits. An eCommerce that depends primarily on paid media to grow is the same — one bad shock and the business is in danger:

- Tariffs (as the US is seeing with China)
- Algorithm changes and privacy shifts (GDPR, iOS)
- New competitors selling at prices far below yours

Early on, it's normal for most sales to come from paid acquisition.

**But as your eCommerce matures, the priority becomes growing the % of returning customers and building brand demand that isn't 100% rented from platforms.**
