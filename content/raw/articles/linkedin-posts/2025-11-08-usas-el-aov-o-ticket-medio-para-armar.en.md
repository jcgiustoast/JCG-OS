Do you use AOV (average order value) to build your eCommerce strategy?

It's a fatal mistake. Three reasons why:

**1. Extremely vulnerable to outliers.**

A handful of wholesale orders with large tickets can artificially inflate your AOV.

Adding 10 orders of €1,000 each lifts your average from €65 to €70 — a dangerous illusion.

**2. Zero information about actual distribution.**

Two eCommerce brands can have an identical €56 AOV:

- eCom A: sells one product at €56
- eCom B: 50% of customers spend €6, the other 50% spend €106

Same AOV. Completely different businesses. Completely different strategies.

**3. Destructive simplification.**

Your business is complex. You have segments that prefer specific products, others that chase discounts on bundles, and dozens of other behaviors.

Reducing all of that to a single number is a massive mistake.

As Todd Rose says in *The End of Average*: *"Any system designed around the average is doomed to fail."*

Translated for eCommerce: any strategy designed solely around AOV is doomed to fail.
