If your marketing agency charges you a % of ad spend… think twice.

Their core incentive is diametrically opposed to your objective as an eCommerce.

Let me explain.

If you own or work in an eCommerce, your goal is to scale — most likely by increasing ad spend — IF AND ONLY IF you're profitable.

Doubling your Meta spend is useless if revenue doesn't move with it. The only outcome there is lower marginal contribution and lower profitability.

But an agency that makes more when you spend more doesn't really care whether you end the month more profitable.

What they want is to convince you that spending more is good for your business — profitable or not.

They win when they spend more. Not when you win.

The same logic applies to agencies charging a % of revenue or a % of platform ROAS:

- Higher revenue ≠ higher profit
- Platform ROAS is easy to manipulate and lies to you

The only way to align your goals with the agency's incentives is for them to charge on marginal contribution — or, if that's not possible, on spend with an efficiency cap (MER, ideally aMER).

That way, they only win when you and your business win.
