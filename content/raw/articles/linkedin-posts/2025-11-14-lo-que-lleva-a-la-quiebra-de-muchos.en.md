What drives a lot of eCommerce brands into bankruptcy isn't in the P&L.

It's in the balance sheet and cash flow statement.

Let me explain.

Many CMOs and CEOs get comfortable when they see a healthy P&L with 10–15% profit. But in a lot of cases, the reality behind that number is completely different.

**Example 1.**

A high P&L profit means nothing if you still have 60% of your inventory sitting unsold. It looks like an asset, but it's cash frozen in the warehouse — and at a lower margin than you'd hoped.

**Example 2.**

If you plan to grow hard through BFCM, you need enough cash on hand to fund the inventory purchase. Poor cash flow management can cap your potential — or in the worst case, push you into bankruptcy.

**Example 3.**

If your inventory takes 60 days to arrive after payment (as it does when producing in China), and you take another 10 days to collect payment from customers, you need cash to finance that timing gap between what you pay and what you collect.

Add interest payments, CapEx, wholesale terms, and debt service, and dozens more examples appear.

That's why you — as CMO or CEO — need to work closely with your CFO and, ideally, with a marketing agency that actually understands this.

Otherwise, the marketing team ends up celebrating a pretty P&L while the CFO is pulling their hair out because ROI is in the basement — thanks to dead inventory in the warehouse.
