You're over-managing Meta and Google Ads.

And instead of helping, you're making it worse.

Sound familiar:

*You launch a new ad set with a cost cap of €20 and a 7-day click attribution window. Two days in, your CPA is €50. You immediately cut the budget and drop the cost target.*

Or: *An ad has spent a lot with low ROAS, so you kill it.*

It doesn't look like it, but this is a very common mistake — and it goes against what both Meta and Google recommend.

We have to learn to trust the algorithm, let it optimize, and not react before the data is real.

If you're using bid caps or cost targets, Meta spends on an ad because it believes it can hit the ROAS or CPA target. Meta has millions of data points helping it decide whether a creative will work now and in the future. You don't. Don't assume your judgment beats the algorithm.

Also: strong past performance doesn't guarantee the same performance in the future — especially at higher budgets. Read *The Breakdown Effect* by Meta.

Most of the time, the right move is to let the algorithm optimize on its own and focus your energy on strategy.
