Growth for eCommerce and SaaS look like the same job on the surface.

The reality is completely different.

Each has its own mechanics, and if you don't understand them, your strategy will walk with a limp.

Three that matter most:

**1. eCommerce carries inventory.**

Every time the business buys stock, it's placing a bet that it can sell through at a higher margin within a specific window.

That's a real risk. Often you don't sell through fast enough, and cash sits stuck in the warehouse instead of buying better-selling SKUs or funding paid media.

A Growth strategy that doesn't account for how to move that inventory is incomplete.

**2. eCommerce has high variable costs.**

Physical products mean production, procurement, shipping, returns, breakage.

SaaS variable cost per additional subscription is near zero — that's why gross margins are so much wider.

If you're running Growth for eCom and you don't know the unit economics per SKU and category, you are bleeding money somewhere.

**3. Sales channels.**

eCommerce isn't just DTC web. It's Amazon, wholesale (Walmart, El Corte Inglés), retail, your own physical stores.

A real eCom Growth strategy accounts for cannibalization between channels, studies their relationships, and exploits the synergies.

---

Growth for SaaS and Growth for eCom are completely different worlds under a shared label. Understanding those particulars is what takes a strategy to the next level.
