Most eCommerce brands forecast like it's astrology.

Marketing and Finance sit down, throw numbers into a spreadsheet based on what they'd like to happen, then save it in a Drive folder nobody opens again.

Three months later, they're 20% behind target and nobody knows why.

The problem isn't that the forecast is wrong. The problem is that they're using it for something it isn't.

A forecast is not a divination exercise. It's a strategy and execution exercise.

And if you expect to grow 30% doing exactly what you did last year, you don't need a forecast.

You need to rethink the tool.
