Shopify and ChatGPT just integrated. And in my view, it's more hype than anything else.

The new feature lets users buy Shopify products inside ChatGPT. Sounds revolutionary. Sounds like it will change eCommerce forever.

It won't. Not yet.

We've seen this movie before:
- Meta Shops
- TikTok Shops
- Google Shopping
- And plenty of others that didn't survive

In most cases, those channels represent at most 10% of total sales. None moved the needle enough. None meaningfully changed buyer behavior.

Why would the ChatGPT integration be different?

This reminds me of the SEO panic. Everyone said AI would kill search. When I look at client and colleague reports, AI-driven traffic is a rounding error compared to Google.

Same story is likely with ChatGPT <> Shopify. Some volume will move, but not enough to transform the ecosystem. At least in the short/mid term.

Long term? I have my doubts. We'll probably lean more on chat interfaces and let them choose for us without thinking twice. But that shift takes years. Behavior doesn't bend on launch day.

In the meantime, focus on what actually compounds:

- Products that solve real needs
- Marketing that lands
- A brand that matters to people

Everything else is secondary.
