In eCommerce, Marketing without Finance is blind.

And Finance without Marketing has no legs.

What do I mean?

When you do marketing without accounting for the financial side, you risk scaling something that is losing money. For example:

- When you set up Meta or Google campaigns, you need to know the variable cost of every product you sell.
- To know the minimum you need to sell, you need to know the fixed costs you have to cover.
- To decide which products to push, you need to know the inventory position of each one to avoid trapping cash in the warehouse.

On the other side, if Finance doesn't talk to Marketing:

- Forecasts get set that are miles away from reality, impossible to hit with the current budget.
- Marketing is seen as a cost center instead of the growth engine of the business.
- The Finance team misses concepts that would help them forecast and decide better — LTV, AOV, ARPU, incrementality, and more.

The eCommerce brands that actually make a difference have Marketing and Finance sitting at the same table, trading ideas, and making decisions together.

Because each one needs the other to be effective.

This is one of the things I'm most passionate about — combining these two worlds.

Marketing-led finance.
Finance-led marketing.

Because in the end, it's the only way either of them has real impact.
