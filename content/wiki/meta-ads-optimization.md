# Meta Ads Optimization

## Meta Andromeda: 7 Strategies

Meta's Andromeda algorithm update changed how ads are distributed and optimized. Seven strategies to maximize it:

1. **Consolidate campaigns** -- fewer campaigns with broader targeting lets the algorithm optimize better
2. **Use Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns** -- leverages Andromeda's full machine learning capabilities
3. **Prioritize creative diversity** -- the algorithm tests and allocates spend to winning creatives faster
4. **Feed the algorithm with quality data** -- Conversions API (CAPI) implementation is critical
5. **Broad targeting over narrow audiences** -- Andromeda performs better with larger audience pools
6. **Creative volume matters** -- deploy more creative variants for the algorithm to test
7. **Let campaigns run** -- avoid premature optimization; give the algorithm time to learn

## 10 Strategies to Optimize Meta Ads ROI

Proven tactics from eCommerce client work:

1. **Implement CAPI correctly** -- server-side tracking ensures Meta receives accurate conversion data despite browser restrictions
2. **Test creative formats aggressively** -- video, static, carousel, UGC. Different formats win for different audiences
3. **Use cost caps / bid controls** -- never give Meta unlimited budget without profitability guardrails (see [[profitability-levers]] lever #2)
4. **Segment by customer type** -- separate campaigns for prospecting vs retargeting with different ROAS targets
5. **Optimize for the right event** -- purchases over add-to-carts; value optimization over conversion optimization when volume allows
6. **Exclude recent buyers** -- prevents inflated attribution from showing ads to people already converting
7. **Test incrementality by channel** -- validate Meta's real impact with Geo Holdouts (see [[measurement-incrementality]])
8. **Creative refresh cadence** -- combat fatigue by rotating creatives every 2-4 weeks
9. **Landing page alignment** -- ad-to-page consistency improves both conversion and quality score
10. **Product feed optimization** -- for catalog campaigns, product titles/images/descriptions directly impact performance

## Making Meta Profitable

The framework for turning Meta from a cost center into a profitability engine:

### The Problem
Most eCommerces evaluate Meta on ROAS alone, which is misleading (see [[measurement-incrementality]]). They spend more when ROAS looks good and cut when it drops -- reactive, not strategic.

### The Solution: Marginal Contribution-Based Approach

1. **Define breakeven ROAS** -- calculate the actual ROAS needed to cover all variable costs and contribute to fixed costs (not the platform number, the real number)
2. **Apply incrementality factor** -- adjust platform ROAS by the tested incrementality factor
3. **Set bid controls** -- implement CPA or ROAS targets that protect margins
4. **Monitor MER and aMER** -- track total efficiency, not just platform reporting
5. **Evaluate LTV, not just first purchase** -- a breakeven first-order ROAS may be highly profitable at 60-day LTV (see [[ltv-frameworks]])

### Key Metrics for Meta Evaluation

| Metric | Source | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Platform ROAS | Meta Ads Manager | Directional only |
| Incrementality Factor | Geo Holdout tests | Adjusts ROAS to reality |
| nCAC | Business data | True acquisition cost |
| aMER | Business data | Acquisition efficiency |
| 60-Day LTV | Cohort analysis | True customer value |
| Marginal Contribution | P&L | Ultimate decision metric |
