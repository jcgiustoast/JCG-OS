# Hook Formulas

> Hooks decide whether anyone reads the rest. Pick 2-3 candidates per topic, draft, A/B in your head, commit. Update this file weekly with what performed. Match hook to [[platform-tone]].

## 1. The Economics Reveal

Show the gap between what platforms/metrics report and what actually happens financially. This is Juan's voice fingerprint — use liberally.

**Formula:** "[Platform/metric] says [reported number]. Reality: [math breakdown] = [actual number]."

**Best on:** Twitter (single or thread-1), LinkedIn (first line), blog (opening)

**Examples:**
- "Platform says ROAS 3.0. Reality: Revenue $100 - Acq $33 - VAT $10 - Product $30 - Shipping $5 - Returns $10 - Processing $5 = $7 margin. That's a 1.33 real ROAS."
- "Your AOV went up 12%. Your Marginal Contribution went down 8%. Here's why."

## 2. Contrarian Premise

Open with what the audience believes that's wrong. Prove it with math.

**Formula:** "[Widely held belief] is wrong. Here's the math."

**Best on:** Twitter, LinkedIn, blog titles

**Examples:**
- "Your ROAS doesn't mean shit."
- "AOV is lying to you."
- "87% of eCommerces optimize AOV for a customer that doesn't exist."

## 3. Framework-First

Name the framework in the hook. Promise the system, not the tactic.

**Formula:** "The [Framework Name]: [what it does in one line]."

**Best on:** LinkedIn long posts, blog titles, Twitter threads

**Examples:**
- "The Metric Tree: 5 branches that replace your KPI dashboard."
- "Powered ICE: how to prioritize experiments by P&L impact, not gut."
- "The Growth Formula: Traffic × CR × AOV × LTV Multiplier - Costs."

## 4. Specificity / Proof

Lead with real numbers at unusual granularity. Specific beats "significant."

**Formula:** "[Specific metric] [unusual timeframe/scope] → [result]"

**Best on:** Twitter single, LinkedIn hook line, case-study openers

**Examples:**
- "We tested 47 subscription offers across 12 months. Here's what worked."
- "$4M brand. 180 creative concepts deployed. CAC dropped 22% in 21 days."
- "First-month churn at 38%. After one cohort redesign: 14%."

## 5. Credential Anchor (Phase 1 ONLY — see [[content-strategy]])

Uses Mars Men credential as attention signal. Must also teach something reusable.

**Formula:** "[Credential] + [reusable insight]."

**Phase 1 OK:** "Head of eCommerce at a 9-figure subscription brand. Here's the diagnostic I run when contribution margin declines."

**Phase 1 NOT OK:** "Day in the life at Mars Men." (no reusable teach = blocked by firewall)

**Deprecation:** This hook loses power Phase 2+. Replace with "Founder of ASTEROI / we generated X concepts for a $4M brand" by Q2 2027.

## Rules

- **Test 2-3 hooks per topic** before committing. Draft them side by side.
- **Hook type must match platform** — a framework-first hook rarely works as a Twitter single; an Economics Reveal is weak as a blog title.
- **Never open with "I think," "I believe," or "In today's..."** — see [[brand-voice]] vocabulary rules.
- **No curiosity gaps without payoff.** "You won't believe what happened next" is banned.
- **Update this file** when a hook consistently outperforms. Remove ones that flop.
