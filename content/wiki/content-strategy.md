# Content Strategy

> This page bridges [[strategy]] (business phases) and [[content-index]] (published content). The content plan is a function of the business strategy — language, topics, and constraints change as ASTEROI evolves.

---

## Core Principle: Content Serves Client Acquisition

Content is not a brand exercise. It exists to generate inbound leads for ASTEROI. Every piece should do one of:
1. Demonstrate deep understanding of the audience's exact problem
2. Prove Juan has solved that problem before (case studies, data, results)
3. Show Juan has a system, not just opinions (frameworks, methodology, tools)
4. Increase repeated exposure (the prospect sees Juan's name 10+ times before reaching out)

---

## Language Decision: English

**English is the only language for Phase 1-2. Spanish is an expansion channel starting Phase 3+.**

Rationale:
- The Mars Men credential ("Head of eComm at a $100M subscription brand backed by L Catterton") only has power with the English-speaking US DTC audience. The Spanish-speaking market doesn't know Mars Men, L Catterton, or why $100M run rate in 18 months is impressive.
- ASTEROI's volume target ($10-15M/year, 60-75 clients at $12-20K/month) requires US-market clients. Those clients are reached through English content.
- Spanish-speaking eComm brands pay $3-5K/month — you'd need 150-250 clients to hit the same revenue. Different business model entirely.
- There's no Taylor Holiday equivalent in Spanish (low competition), but the TAM doesn't justify the opportunity cost during Phase 1-2 when Juan's time is maximally constrained.

**Spanish becomes valuable in Phase 3+ (2028+) for a specific niche:** LATAM/Spanish brands entering the US market. At that point, repurpose the best English content — not 2x the work, more like 1.3x.

---

## The Mars Men Credential: Powerful but Depreciating

"Head of eCommerce at a brand that went from zero to $100M run rate in under 18 months."

This is a 12-month asset (Phase 1 only). After leaving Mars Men, it becomes "former Head of eComm" — still useful but decaying.

**Strategy:** Use it to get attention NOW, but build a body of work that stands on its own after Juan leaves. The content must compound independently of the job title.

| Content type | Uses Mars Men credential | Outlives Mars Men |
|-------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| "Here's what I learned at a $100M brand" | Yes — attention grabber | Weak — becomes stale after departure |
| "Here's the framework I use to diagnose declining contribution margin" | Yes — credibility boost | Strong — the framework is valuable regardless of employer |
| "We tested 47 subscription offers across 12 months. Here's what works." | Yes — proves scale | Strong — the data and insights stand alone |
| "This $4M brand was spending $60K/month with random creatives. We deployed 180 AI concepts. CAC dropped 22%." | No — ASTEROI proof point | Very strong — proof of ASTEROI's value, not Mars Men's |

**Rule:** Every piece that uses Mars Men must also teach something reusable. No pure "look at my cool job" content.

## Phase 1: "The Stealth Build" (April 2026 - Q1 2027)

### Constraints
- Cannot mention Mars Men by name (firewall — see [[projects]])
- Cannot discuss Meta Ads, creative strategy, ad spend management (Raheel's domain)
- Cannot position as ASTEROI founder publicly
- Must look like "thought leader sharing expertise," not "agency owner selling services"

### Approved Topics
From [[strategy]]:
- CRO and experimentation methodology
- Subscription eCommerce economics (churn, LTV, cohort analysis)
- eCommerce finance and unit economics
- Retention and growth strategy
- Frameworks: Powered ICE, Marginal Contribution, Metric Tree, Growth Formula

### Channels & Cadence

| Channel | Frequency | Language | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Twitter/X | 3-5 tweets/week | English | Short, opinionated takes. Build US DTC audience. High volume, low effort per piece. |
| LinkedIn | 1-2 posts/week | English | Longer-form. Repurpose existing 37 articles. Professional credibility. |
| jcgiusto.com | 1 deep piece/month | English | SEO, credibility, linkable asset. Evergreen frameworks. |
| ASTEROI blog | **PAUSED** | -- | Don't promote agency while stealth. Resume in Phase 2. |

### Content That Converts (What Actually Drives ASTEROI Revenue)
1. **Specificity posts:** "We tested 47 subscription offers..." — shows depth no generalist has
2. **Framework posts:** "Here's the exact diagnostic I run when contribution margin declines" — proves system
3. **Contrarian takes:** "ROAS doesn't mean shit" (already published) — earns attention + filters for right audience
4. **Mini case studies:** Anonymized results from ASTEROI clients (once available)

### What NOT to Post
- "Day in the life at a $100M brand" vanity content
- Meta Ads tactics, creative breakdowns, ad spend strategy
- Anything mentioning Mars Men, Raheel, Zach, Benjamin by name
- ASTEROI service promotions or CTAs
- Content in Spanish (not this phase)

## Phase 2: "The Transition" (Q2 2027 - Q4 2027)

### Constraints Lifted
- Can discuss Meta Ads, creative strategy, full-stack growth
- Can position as ASTEROI co-founder publicly
- Mars Men experience becomes anonymized case study material ("a $100M subscription brand I led eComm for")

### What Changes
- **Topic expansion:** Full-stack growth including Meta Ads, creative strategy, AI-native tooling
- **Identity shift:** "Juan Cruz, operator at a $100M brand" → "Juan Cruz, founder of ASTEROI"
- **Proof points shift:** Mars Men credential → ASTEROI client results + AI tooling demonstrations
- ASTEROI blog restarts with Juan as public voice
- Possible additions: podcast, video content, conference talks

### Channels & Cadence

| Channel | Frequency | Language | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Twitter/X | 5-7 tweets/week | English | Increase volume. Now includes Meta Ads + creative topics. |
| LinkedIn | 2-3 posts/week | English | Founder positioning. Case studies. |
| jcgiusto.com | 1-2 deep pieces/month | English | SEO + thought leadership |
| ASTEROI blog | 2 posts/month | English | Agency credibility. Client-facing content. |

### Key Content Shift
The AI execution loop becomes the signature proof point:
> "We generated 200 creative concepts and deployed the best 20 to Meta in 3 days for a $4M brand. CAC dropped 22% in the first cycle."

This replaces Mars Men as the primary credibility signal. It's more powerful because it's ASTEROI-specific and demonstrates a capability no one else has.

## Phase 3+: Spanish Expansion (2028+)

### When to Add Spanish
- ASTEROI has 20-30 clients and real English-language case studies
- Juan's personal brand is established in the English DTC space
- There's a clear segment of LATAM/Spanish brands entering the US that need ASTEROI's services

### How to Add It Efficiently
- Repurpose the top-performing English content (translate + adapt, not create from scratch)
- ~1.3x the effort, not 2x
- Target: LATAM/Spanish brands selling into the US or cross-border (not domestic Spanish eComm — pricing too low)
- Channel: LinkedIn (Spanish), possibly Twitter/X in Spanish

### What Not to Do
- Don't create original Spanish-only content (wastes effort, splits focus)
- Don't target domestic Spanish eComm market as primary (pricing ceiling too low for ASTEROI's volume model)
