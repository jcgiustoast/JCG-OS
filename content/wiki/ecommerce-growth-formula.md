# eCommerce Growth Formula

## The Core Formula

**GROWTH = (TRAFFIC x CONVERSION RATE x AOV x 60-DAY LTV MULTIPLIER) - VARIABLE COSTS - FIXED COSTS**

This equation is the foundational framework for scaling eCommerce businesses profitably through strategic experimentation. Each variable is an experimentation surface.

## Breaking Down Each Variable

### Traffic
Cost and performance of visitor acquisition:
- **CPM** (Cost Per Mille) -- expense per thousand impressions
- **CTR** (Click-Through Rate) -- ad engagement
- **Incrementality** -- authentic marketing impact beyond platform attribution (see [[measurement-incrementality]])

Optimization areas: creative quality (videos, images, copy), audience targeting, sales messaging angles. The goal is understanding which approaches deliver superior cost-to-result ratios.

### Conversion Rate
Total buyers / total users. Varies significantly by industry.

Optimization areas:
- **Funnel analysis** -- identifying dropout stages between session start and purchase
- **Ad-landing page consistency** -- visual and messaging alignment
- **Segment performance** -- detecting underperforming demographics or channels
- **Mobile performance gaps** and geographic conversion disparities

### AOV (Average Order Value)
How much a customer spends per order on average. AOV improvement equals conversion rate improvements in impact.

Optimization strategies:
- Pricing adjustments (see [[profitability-levers]] lever #1: price testing)
- Upsell and cross-sell tactics
- Shipping threshold modifications

Higher AOV enables increased marketing spend capacity, creating competitive advantages in acquisition.

**Warning:** AOV as a metric has serious limitations. See [[ecommerce-metrics-hierarchy]] for why histogram analysis and segmentation beat simple averages.

### 60-Day LTV Multiplier
Customer spending within 60 days post-purchase. See [[ltv-frameworks]] for deep dive.

**Example:** Cohort acquired in August with $50 AOV reaching $75 average LTV by October = 1.5x multiplier.

Enhancement approaches:
- Subscription offerings
- Post-purchase flow optimization
- Communication expansion (apps, SMS)
- Membership or affiliate programs

### Variable Costs
Scale proportionally with revenue:
- Cost of Goods Sold
- Payment processing fees
- Shipping expenses
- Return costs

**Key insight:** "A 2% decrease in variable costs has the same effect as a 2% increase in ARPU."

**Case study:** HexClad reduced bundle cookware from 13 to 12 units without price reduction, maintaining ARPU while improving gross margins.

### Fixed Costs
Non-proportional expenses: customer service, rent, personnel, marketing software.

Experimentation opportunities:
- A/B testing AI vs human support
- FAQ improvement reducing support tickets
- Evaluating incremental revenue from affiliate or SMS platforms

## The eCommerce Growth Code: 5 Principles

A higher-level strategic framework that contextualizes the formula:

1. **Know your numbers** -- financial literacy across the team, not just finance
2. **Measure what matters** -- use the metrics hierarchy (see [[ecommerce-metrics-hierarchy]]), not vanity metrics
3. **Experiment systematically** -- apply the scientific method to every business dimension (see [[experimentation-frameworks]])
4. **Optimize profitability, not just revenue** -- marginal contribution as North Star (see [[profitability-levers]])
5. **Build systems, not campaigns** -- sustainable infrastructure over one-off wins

## The Formula as Diagnostic Tool

When growth stalls, the formula pinpoints where:
- **Traffic declining?** --> Creative fatigue, rising CPMs, audience saturation
- **Conversion dropping?** --> Funnel friction, ad-page misalignment, mobile issues
- **AOV shrinking?** --> Product mix, discount dependency, segment shifts
- **LTV multiplier declining?** --> Retention issues, post-purchase flow gaps
- **Variable costs rising?** --> COGS inflation, return rate increase, shipping costs
- **Fixed costs growing faster than revenue?** --> Operational bloat
