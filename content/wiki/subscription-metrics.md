# Subscription Metrics & The Metric Tree

> No metric exists in isolation. Changes cascade through the system in sometimes unexpected ways.

## The Metric Tree Framework

The Subscription eCommerce Metric Tree maps how metrics interconnect and drive profitability. Every branch grows from a single root.

### The Root: Contribution Margin

**CONTRIBUTION MARGIN = REVENUE - VARIABLE COSTS**

This determines whether a business can scale profitably or merely transfers money between pockets. See [[profitability-levers]] for how to optimize it.

### Branch 1: Acquisition & Average Profit per User

Measures ability to bring in new customers profitably.

**ARPU = AOV x Conversion Rate**

Key sub-metrics:
- **Subscription Take Rate** — A 5% increase translates to 15-25% more lifetime value (subscribers generate 2.5-4x more revenue than one-time purchasers)
- **Prepaid Plan Take Rate** — 30-50% better retention vs monthly subscribers
- **Gift Take Rate** — 15-25% of revenue for mature brands
- **Average Units per Order** — Increasing from 1.2 to 1.5 typically yields 12-18% higher margin contribution
- **Non-sub AOV vs Sub AOV** — Healthy businesses maintain subscriber AOV at 85-105% of one-time purchaser AOV despite subscription discounts

**Cost side:**
- Shipping Cost: 8-15% of revenue; 10% reduction yields 1-2% higher contribution margin
- COGS: typically 25-45% of revenue
- Discount %: top performers maintain 10-20% average discount levels

### Branch 2: Activation & 60-Day Lifetime Profit

Critical first 60 days for new subscribers.

**Subscriber Activation Metrics:**
- **1st Renewal Rate** — most critical renewal metric
- **2nd Renewal Rate** — significant long-term indicator
- **3rd Renewal Rate** — by this point, subscribers show much stronger retention
- **Delay Rate** — % pushing back deliveries
- **Pause Rate** — % temporarily halting
- **Customer Portal Use Rate** — active management correlates with 42% better retention
- **Positive Portal Interaction Rate** — positive engagers show longer retention

**Non-Subscriber Activation:**
- Subscribed on Returning Order %
- Days to 2nd Order
- Returning Rate

**Data point:** A home goods subscription reduced first-month churn by 34% through a "portal tour" email sent 48 hours after sign-up.

### Branch 3: Retention & 180/360-Day Lifetime Profit

Long-term subscriber base health. See [[ltv-frameworks]] for LTV measurement.

**Churn Taxonomy:**
- **Passive Churn** — 20-30% of total churn; recovery efforts yield 3-4x ROI
- **Active Churn** — deliberate cancellations; even 0.5% monthly reduction yields hundreds of thousands annually
- **Pause-to-Churn** — 40-60% of paused subscriptions eventually cancel without proper management

**Recovery Metrics:**
- **Payment Recovery Rate** — 60-75% of failed payments recovered by top performers
- **Cancel Flow Save Rate** — converts 15-30% of cancellation attempts into pauses/continued subscriptions
- **Reactivation Rate** — reactivated subscribers cost 30-40% less than new customers with 10-15% better retention

**Data point:** Personalizing cancel flow based on cancellation reasons increased save rate from 8% to 24%.

### Branch 4: Revenue Expansion

How existing customers grow in value over time.

- **Add-on Purchase Rate** — 30-45% of subscribers add products quarterly, contributing 15-25% additional revenue
- **Subscription Upgrade Rate** — 1% increase yields 3-5% more annual revenue
- **Cross-sell Conversion Rate** — customers buying across 2+ categories show 40-60% higher LTV
- **Upsell Conversion Rate** — premium products carry 1.5-2x higher gross profit margins

**Data point:** Quick-add buttons in order notification emails increased add-on purchases by 9%.

### Branch 5: Referral Amplification

Where customers become advocates.

- NPS 9-10 scorers are 4-6x more likely to refer than 7-8 scorers
- 15-30% of satisfied customers actively participate in referral programs
- Referred customers convert 3-5x higher with 15-25% better retention
- Two-sided referral programs doubled referral rates

## How Metrics Flow Through the Tree

1. Higher subscription take rates --> better first renewal rates
2. Portal interaction in first 30 days correlates with 42% better retention
3. Customers rarely upgrade before fourth renewal (retention enables expansion)
4. Best referrers are customers who upgraded or added products

## Experimentation Priority Order

1. **Strengthen roots** — optimize COGS and shipping costs
2. **Develop primary branches** — perfect subscription take rate and defaults
3. **Nurture early growth** — optimize onboarding and first renewal
4. **Strengthen mature branches** — improve cancel flow and reactivation
5. **Harvest fruit** — launch upsells, cross-sells, and referral programs

See [[experimentation-frameworks]] for how to prioritize and design these experiments.
