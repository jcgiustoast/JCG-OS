# Audience: Operators

## Who They Are

CMOs, Heads of eCommerce, VPs of Growth, senior performance marketers at 7-9 figure DTC brands. Finance-literate — they read P&Ls, know what Marginal Contribution is, already skeptical of ROAS. Revenue range: their brand does $5M-$100M/yr. Already building systems, not looking for 101 content.

## What They Want

- **Math they haven't seen** — the Economics Reveal that changes how they model their own P&L
- **Frameworks they can deploy Monday morning** — Powered ICE, Metric Tree, Growth Formula
- **Proof from similar-scale brands** — "a 9-figure subscription brand" resonates; $4M brand proof is table-stakes
- **Systems over tactics** — they've read every "10 hacks" post; they want methodology
- **Tools with context** — not "use X," but "here's when X breaks and when Y replaces it"

## How to Talk to Them

- Direct and specific. "Here's 5 steps" not "consider exploring."
- Show the math. Real screenshots, real numbers, real process.
- Peer energy — Juan is building alongside them, not above them.
- Challenge them with Economics Reveals: "if you're reporting ROAS 3.0 and your contribution margin is still dropping, here's why."
- Reference frameworks by name — they'll recognize or Google, both good.

## Platform Fit

- **Twitter:** primary audience (DTC Twitter is operator Twitter)
- **LinkedIn:** secondary (they're on LinkedIn but scroll less)
- **Blog:** primary for evergreen framework SEO

## What They Reject

- Motivational/guru tone
- Acronym soup without definitions
- "Growth hack" energy
- Content that implies they're stupid for not already knowing
