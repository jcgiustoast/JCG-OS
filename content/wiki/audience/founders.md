# Audience: Founders

## Who They Are

Founders and CEOs of 7-8 figure DTC brands ($3M-$30M/yr). Revenue range where they've outgrown freelancers but haven't built full in-house growth teams. They hire agencies, they sign the contracts, they hold the budget. Less technical than [[operators]] — they understand P&L but don't necessarily run cohort analyses themselves.

## What They Want

- **Confidence that the operator knows what they're doing** — frameworks + data + case study structure reads as credibility
- **Signals of rigor over signals of cleverness** — "we tested 47 offers over 12 months" beats "here's a wild hack"
- **Specific outcomes attributable to process** — "CAC dropped 22% in 21 days after deploying 180 concepts" not "we grew a brand"
- **Systems they can delegate** — they're not going to implement Powered ICE themselves; they want to hire someone who runs it
- **Post-hiring confidence** — content proves the hire is safe before the call ever happens

## How to Talk to Them

- Framework-named anchors ("Powered ICE," "Metric Tree") function as proof of IP even if they don't memorize the framework
- Credential Anchor hooks work best here (Phase 1) — "Head of eComm at a 9-figure subscription brand" does heavy lifting
- Less dense math than [[operators]] content, but keep the numbers — they skim, but numbers register
- Narrative framing works: "3 months ago, this brand was losing money on ROAS 3.0. Here's what changed."

## Platform Fit

- **LinkedIn:** primary audience (founders live on LinkedIn; budgets get decided here)
- **Blog:** primary — content lives long enough to surface in "who should I hire" searches
- **Twitter:** secondary (they lurk more than post)

## What They Reject

- Content that talks down to them technically
- Hype/guru tone — they've been pitched by 50 agencies already
- Service CTAs (Phase 1 firewall aligns with this anyway — the absence of a pitch IS the pitch)
- Vague case studies — "we helped a brand grow" without numbers reads as no proof

## Phase Evolution

- **Phase 1:** Juan is "expert operator," founders notice but don't know he's buildable yet
- **Phase 2:** Juan becomes "founder of ASTEROI" — founders are now the direct buyers, content shifts to ASTEROI case studies with real client proof
- **Phase 3:** Spanish expansion targets LATAM founders entering US market — same audience profile, different cultural entry point
