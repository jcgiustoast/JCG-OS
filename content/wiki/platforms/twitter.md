# Twitter / X

## Platform DNA

- 280 chars per tweet; long-form up to 25,000 (sweet spot 500-1,500 for threads)
- Fast scroll — 0.5 seconds to stop someone
- Audience skews builder/operator/DTC-Twitter
- Primary audience: [[operators]]. Secondary: [[founders]].

## Phase 1 Firewall (see [[content-strategy]])

- No Mars Men / Raheel / Zach / Benjamin by name
- No Meta Ads tactics, creative strategy, ad spend
- No ASTEROI mentions, no service CTAs
- Every piece must teach something reusable — no pure vanity posts

## Content Rules

- Lead with a hook from [[hooks]] — Contrarian Premise, Economics Reveal, or Specificity work best on X
- Voice per [[brand-voice]], adapted per [[platform-tone]] (normal sentence case, compressed)
- Line breaks between thoughts — never dense paragraphs
- No hashtags in body, ever
- No "Thread:" or "1/" numbering — use natural breaks
- No "follow for more" engagement bait
- Links go in a reply, not the main tweet

## Formats

### A) Single tweet (hot take / insight)
One or two sentences. Contrarian or surprising. Must be independently valuable.

Example: "87% of eCommerces optimize AOV for a customer that doesn't exist. The average is a statistical ghost."

### B) Short thread (3-7 tweets)
- Tweet 1: Hook (must stand alone)
- Tweets 2-5: Economics Reveal or framework breakdown, one idea per tweet, numbers every tweet
- Final tweet: Framework name or prescription. "Save this" energy, no service CTA.
- Each tweet independently valuable (could be quote-tweeted)

### C) Deep thread (8-12 tweets)
Full framework explanation with examples. Break at thought boundaries, not mid-sentence.

### D) Long-form tweet (1,000-2,000 chars)
One extended argument. Use sparingly, 1x/week max. Good for Economics Reveal with math.

## Posting Strategy

- Frequency: 3-5 tweets/week (Phase 1), scaling to 5-7 in Phase 2
- Best times: 8-9am, 12-1pm, 5-6pm ET
- Engage in replies for 30 min after posting
- See [[content-strategy]] for phase cadence

## Repurposing Notes

- In Juan's [[repurpose]] chain, Twitter is usually the **compression** step, not the seed — after writing the blog or LinkedIn long-post, distill the core framework into a thread.
- Exception: Hot takes and contrarian singles can originate on Twitter and expand outward.
- When distilling from LinkedIn: strip the narrative, keep the math and the framework name.
- Cross-post adapted hooks to future [[threads]] node (Phase 2+).
