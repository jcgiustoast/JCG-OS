# LinkedIn

## Platform DNA

- Sweet spot 1,300-2,000 chars for posts; up to 3,000 for articles
- Professional but human — personal "I" stories with business lessons
- Higher-ticket audience than Twitter (decision-makers with budgets)
- Primary audience: [[founders]]. Secondary: [[operators]].

## Phase 1 Firewall (see [[content-strategy]])

- No Mars Men / Raheel / Zach / Benjamin by name
- No Meta Ads tactics, creative strategy, ad spend
- Credential OK as: "Head of eCommerce at a 9-figure subscription brand" or "a $100M+ DTC brand backed by L Catterton"
- No ASTEROI founder positioning until Phase 2
- Every piece must teach a reusable framework or insight

## Content Rules

- First line is everything — LinkedIn truncates after ~210 chars with "see more." If the hook isn't in that first line, nobody clicks.
- Lead with hook from [[hooks]] — Framework-First and Specificity perform best on LinkedIn; Credential Anchor works in Phase 1.
- Voice per [[brand-voice]], adapted per [[platform-tone]] (more narrative, still direct)
- Heavy line breaks — one idea per paragraph
- No hashtags in body; 3-5 at the very end separated by a line break
  - Suggested: #ecommerce #dtc #subscriptionecommerce #cro #experimentation #uniteconomics
- Links in the first comment, never in the post body
- Close with a specific question that shows expertise — not "Agree? Thoughts?"

## Formats

### A) Short post (500-800 chars)
One sharp insight with a data point or framework reference. 3-5 short paragraphs, heavy line breaks. Opens with Economics Reveal or Contrarian Premise.

### B) Standard post (1,000-1,800 chars)
Structure: Hook → Problem statement → Economics reveal or framework → Prescription → Question.
Can include a simple list (3-5 items) or before/after comparison.

### C) Long post (1,800-3,000 chars)
Full framework breakdown or anonymized case study.
Structure: Hook → Context → Problem → Reveal → Framework → Application → Question.

## Posting Strategy

- Frequency: 1-2 posts/week (Phase 1), scaling to 2-3 in Phase 2
- Best times: 7-8am, 12pm, 5-6pm ET (business hours)
- Comment on 10 relevant posts before publishing (engagement lift)
- See [[content-strategy]] for phase cadence

## Repurposing Notes

- In Juan's [[repurpose]] chain, LinkedIn often seeds the piece when the topic is narrative or credential-anchored.
- When expanding from a Twitter thread: add personal narrative framing, expand each tweet into a paragraph, rewrite the hook (what works on X rarely lands on LinkedIn).
- When compressing from blog: extract one framework or one Economics Reveal, keep the numbers, drop the subheadings.
