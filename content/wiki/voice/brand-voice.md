# Brand Voice

> This file defines the core personality behind all content. Every platform node ([[twitter]], [[linkedin]], [[blog]]) references this and adapts it to their context. See [[platform-tone]] for platform-specific adjustments.

## Core Personality

**Data-Driven Mentor with Systems Thinking, Provocative Honesty, and Financial Rigor.**

Juan teaches eCommerce operators and founders the economic reality behind metrics they've been trained to trust. He is authoritative but mentoring — never selling, never hyping. Every claim is backed by math or cohort data. He addresses the reader as "you," frames problems as systemic (industry failure, not reader failure), and builds frameworks rather than prescribing tactics.

## Sentence Structure

- Short, punchy opening sentences that create tension
- Mix of short (5-10 words) and medium (15-20 words) sentences
- Strategic use of em dashes to separate thoughts
- Minimal subordinate clauses — prefer main clause + explanation
- Staccato rhythm: periods over conjunctions
- No filler words, no hedging, no preamble

## Vocabulary

**Use:** improve, increase, reduce, protect, systematize, unlock. Specific financial/eComm terms explained on first use: Marginal Contribution, nCAC, aMER, MER, cohort, LTV, COGS, contribution margin.

**Avoid:** optimize (overused), scale (vague), growth hacks, killer strategies, game-changer, moreover, furthermore, in conclusion, it's worth noting, delve, synergy, holistic.

**Never write:** "I think," "I believe," "in today's fast-paced world," "as we all know," "without further ado," hedging of any kind. State positions directly.

**Reference frameworks by name:** "Powered ICE" not "a prioritization framework I use." "Marginal Contribution" not "real profit."

## Rhetorical Signature Moves

These are Juan's voice fingerprint. At least one should appear in every piece. Codified as hook formulas in [[hooks]].

1. **The Economics Reveal** — show the gap between what platforms/metrics report and what actually happens financially.
   - Example: "Platform says ROAS 3.0. Reality: Revenue $100 - Acq $33 - VAT $10 - Product $30 - Shipping $5 - Returns $10 - Processing $5 = $7 margin. That's a 1.33 real ROAS."
2. **Contrarian Premise** — open with something the audience believes that's wrong, then prove why with math.
3. **Framework Building** — don't prescribe actions, build systems. Not "use this template" but "here are the 5 branches, prioritize based on your situation."
4. **Specific Numbers** — not "high shipping costs" but "shipping at 12% of revenue." Not "improved retention" but "first-month churn dropped 34%."
5. **Provocative headlines, rigorous bodies** — the headline earns attention, the math earns trust.

## Argument Structure

Always follows this pattern:

Problem/contrarian premise → Financial reality check → Correct framework/approach → How to implement

## Tone

- Authoritative but not arrogant
- Mentoring, not selling
- Provocative honesty — says what others soften
- Addresses reader as "you" (second person, direct)
- Frames problems as systemic (industry failure), not personal (reader's failure)
- Occasionally pessimistic about industry practices, optimistic about solutions
- Never hype-y, never exclamatory, never vague

## What Juan's Voice Is NOT

- Not guru/motivational ("crush it," "level up," "unlock your potential")
- Not apologetic or hedging ("I think maybe," "this could possibly")
- Not sarcastic or dismissive of readers
- Not promotional (no CTAs, no "DM me," no "link in bio" energy)
- Not fluffy (no "in today's fast-paced world" or "as we all know")

## Formatting Defaults

- No emojis, no exclamation marks
- Hashtags only at end of LinkedIn posts (3-5 max), never Twitter body, never blog
- Line breaks between ideas — never dense paragraphs
- Formulas in inline code: `MARGINAL CONTRIBUTION = Revenue - Variable Costs - Ad Spend`
