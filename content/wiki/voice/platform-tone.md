# Platform Tone Adaptations

> Core voice defined in [[brand-voice]]. This file defines how that voice ADAPTS per platform. Change tone first, then format, then hook. The voice stays the same — only delivery changes.

## Twitter / X

- Most compressed version of the voice
- Sentence fragments acceptable. Periods over conjunctions.
- Contrarian premise or Economics Reveal goes in tweet 1 — must earn the click alone
- No lowercase-everything (Juan is not a casual lowercase poster; that breaks the authority signal). Normal sentence case.
- Numbers and formulas carry the weight; no adjectives doing persuasion work
- Example: "87% of eCommerces optimize AOV for a customer that doesn't exist. The average is a statistical ghost."

## LinkedIn

- Professional but still human, never corporate
- "I" used as framing for lessons/insights — not "day in the life"
- Longer sentences okay. More narrative scaffolding permitted.
- Heavy line breaks — LinkedIn collapses dense text
- First line is everything. Must hook before the "...see more" cutoff (~210 chars)
- Closes with a specific question that shows expertise, not "Agree? Thoughts?"
- Example: "Your ROAS is lying to you. Here's the real math behind a 3.0 ROAS that's actually losing money."

## Blog (jcgiusto.com)

- Most structured and rigorous
- Framework-naming titles: "The [Framework]: [What it does]" or contrarian claim
- Subheadings every 200-400 words
- Include comparison tables (reported vs reality, before vs after)
- Inline formulas, bolded key phrases for scannability
- No author bio CTA — the framework IS the value

## Deferred Platforms (Phase 2+)

- **ASTEROI blog** (Phase 2): same rigor as jcgiusto.com but with ASTEROI-client proof points replacing Mars Men credential
- **Newsletter** (Phase 2+): most personal tone, behind-the-scenes context, direct-to-inbox intimacy
- **Spanish** (Phase 3+): adapt top-performing English, don't create original

## The Rule

When adapting across platforms: change the **tone** first, then the **format**, then the **hook**. The voice (from [[brand-voice]]) stays the same. If the LinkedIn version could be a Twitter thread with line breaks added, you've reformatted — not rethought. See [[repurpose]] for the chain.
